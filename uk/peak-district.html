🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
517-metre hill with Bronze Age hillfort summit. Free access. Circular ridge walk offers stunning views across Hope Valley and Edale. Called "Shivering Mountain" due to landslips. Popular moderate walk taking 2-3 hours. Can be windy at summit. Beautiful sunrise and sunset spot. Part of Great Ridge walk to Lose Hill. Parking at Mam Nick or Castleton. Allow 2-3 hours. One of Peak District's finest viewpoints.

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Highest point in Peak District at 636 metres. Free access. Plateau famous for 1932 Mass Trespass establishing right to roam. Multiple walking routes varying difficulty. Kinder Downfall waterfall blows backwards in strong wind. Moorland navigation skills required - can be boggy. Beautiful gritstone edges. Weather changes rapidly. Popular with hikers. Allow 4-6 hours for circular walks. Historic importance in access to countryside.

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Four-mile long gritstone cliff popular with climbers. Free access. Easy walking along top of edge with stunning views. Popular climbing location - hundreds of routes. Millstones scattered along edge from historical quarrying. Beautiful moorland setting. Parking at various points along edge. Good for walking without climbing. Pride and Prejudice filmed here. Allow 2-3 hours walking. Can combine with nearby Higger Tor.

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Magnificent stately home of Duke and Duchess of Devonshire. State rooms contain art collection including works by Rembrandt and Van Dyck. Beautiful Capability Brown parkland with 105-acre garden. Cascade waterfall and Emperor Fountain. Farmyard and adventure playground for families. Working estate with shops and restaurants. Allow full day to explore house, gardens, and parkland. One of Britain's finest stately homes. Pre-booking essential.

Bakewell

Market Town Famous for Pudding

Market town on River Wye known for Bakewell Pudding (not tart). Free to explore. Monday market operates since 1254. Old House Museum shows local histo...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Historic market town on the River Wye, the largest settlement within the Peak District National Park and gateway to the White Peak limestone country. Famous for the Bakewell Pudding — not to be confused with the Bakewell Tart — which originated here by accident in the 1820s when a cook at the Rutland Arms misunderstood instructions and spread egg mixture on top of jam instead of mixing it into the pastry. The Old Original Bakewell Pudding Shop and rival establishments each claim the authentic recipe. The five-arched medieval bridge over the Wye dates to around 1200. Monday market has operated since 1254 under a royal charter. The town serves as a base for walks to Chatsworth, Haddon Hall, Monsal Dale, and the Monsal Trail along a former railway line.

Castleton

Village Beneath Peveril Castle

Village in Hope Valley below ruined Norman castle. Free to explore village. Four show caves - Peak Cavern, Speedwell Cavern, Treak Cliff Cavern, Blue ...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Village at the head of the Hope Valley beneath the ruined Peveril Castle, surrounded by four famous show caves and dramatic limestone scenery. Peak Cavern — locally known as the Devil's Arse — has the largest cave entrance in Britain at 20 metres high. Treak Cliff Cavern is the only place in the world where Blue John fluorite is mined, a semi-precious mineral unique to this hillside. Speedwell Cavern is reached by underground boat along a flooded lead mine. The village sits where the White Peak limestone meets the Dark Peak gritstone, creating the striking geology visible at Winnats Pass — a narrow gorge with near-vertical walls formed by a collapsed cavern system. Mam Tor above provides panoramic views across the Hope Valley.

Peak Cavern

Britain's Largest Cave Entrance

Largest cave entrance in Britain - 20 metres high, 30 metres wide. Guided tours explore underground river and caverns. Rope-makers worked in entrance ...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Largest cave entrance in Britain - 20 metres high, 30 metres wide. Guided tours explore underground river and caverns. Rope-makers worked in entrance until early 1900s. Beautiful limestone formations. Tours take about 1 hour. Moderate walking inside cave. Can be cold and wet - dress accordingly. Pre-booking recommended. Near Castleton village centre. Good family attraction. Interesting history and geology.
