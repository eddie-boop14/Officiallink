King's College Chapel

Gothic Masterpiece with World-Famous Choir

Completed 1515 after 100 years of construction. Considered finest example of late Perpendicular Gothic architecture in England. Features world's large...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Completed 1515 after 100 years of construction. Considered finest example of late Perpendicular Gothic architecture in England. Features world's largest fan-vaulted ceiling and spectacular stained glass windows. King's College Choir performs here - famous for Festival of Nine Lessons and Carols broadcast globally each Christmas Eve. Rubens' Adoration of the Magi hangs above altar. Stunning acoustics. Allow 1 hour minimum.

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
-Cambridge's Founded 1546 by Henry VIII. Largest and wealthiest Cambridge college. Great Court is largest enclosed courtyard in Europe. Famous alumni include Isaac Newton, Lord Byron, and 34 Nobel Prize winners. Newton's apple tree descendant grows in grounds. Wren Library houses A.A. Milne's Winnie-the-Pooh manuscripts and Newton's papers. Working academic college. Visitor access varies during term time.

St John's College

Tudor Foundation with Bridge of Sighs

Founded 1511 by Lady Margaret Beaufort, mother of Henry VII. Bridge of Sighs (1831) crosses River Cam connecting old and new courts - named after Veni...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Founded 1511 by Lady Margaret Beaufort, mother of Henry VII. Bridge of Sighs (1831) crosses River Cam connecting old and new courts - named after Venice's bridge. College owns second-most land in Cambridge after Trinity. Chapel features beautiful fan vaulting. Famous alumni include William Wordsworth. College spans both sides of River Cam with extensive grounds. Beautiful wedding-cake architecture on New Court.

🌍 Maps ✅
Scenic green spaces behind colleges along River Cam. Best views of King's College, Clare College, and Trinity College from river. Free to walk. Popular punting route passes through. Spring daffodils and crocuses spectacular. Summer sees students and tourists picnicking. Accessible via several bridges including Mathematical Bridge and Bridge of Sighs. Classic Cambridge photographs taken here. Allow 30-60 minutes strolling.

Fitzwilliam Museum

Art and Antiquities Museum

University of Cambridge's principal museum, founded 1816. Collections span antiquities from Egypt, Greece, Rome, plus European paintings, sculpture, c...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
University of Cambridge's principal museum, founded 1816. Collections span antiquities from Egypt, Greece, Rome, plus European paintings, sculpture, ceramics, and illuminated manuscripts. Works by Titian, Rubens, Monet, and Picasso. Ancient Egyptian sarcophagi and Greek vases. Beautiful neo-classical building. Special exhibitions may require tickets. Allow 2-3 hours minimum. One of Britain's finest regional museums.

Mathematical Bridge

Wooden Bridge at Queens' College

Wooden bridge across River Cam at Queens' College, built 1749. Named for mathematical precision of its tangent and radial trussed design. Legend claim...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Wooden bridge across River Cam at Queens' College, built 1749. Named for mathematical precision of its tangent and radial trussed design. Legend claims it was built without bolts by Newton - actually built 22 years after his death and always used bolts. Rebuilt twice maintaining original design. Best viewed from punts on river or from Silver Street Bridge. Part of Queens' College so viewable from outside only unless visiting college.

Corpus Clock

Unique Mechanical Grasshopper Clock

Unveiled 2008 outside Corpus Christi College on Trumpington Street. Large golden clock with mechanical grasshopper (chronophage - time eater) on top. ...

🌍 Maps ✅
Unveiled 2008 outside Corpus Christi College on Trumpington Street. Large golden clock with mechanical grasshopper (chronophage - time eater) on top. Grasshopper moves along eating seconds. Clock displays time through opening and closing slits but deliberately irregular to represent life's irregular nature. Created by inventor John Taylor. Free to view 24/7 from street. Popular photo stop. Represents time devouring everything.

Punting on the Cam

Traditional Cambridge Boat Experience

Quintessential Cambridge activity propelling flat-bottomed boat with pole. Main punting from Mill Lane, Magdalene Bridge, or Quayside. Route passes Ki...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Quintessential Cambridge activity propelling flat-bottomed boat with pole. Main punting from Mill Lane, Magdalene Bridge, or Quayside. Route passes King's College, Clare College, backs of colleges, under bridges. Hire by hour or take guided chauffeur tour. Cambridge style punts from platform (unlike Oxford). Popular spring through autumn. Book ahead during summer. Takes practice - expect to get wet learning. Beautiful perspective of colleges from water.

Parker's Piece

Historic Common - Football Rules Birthplace

25-acre common in city centre where Cambridge Rules of football were established 1848 - precursor to modern association football. Large flat green spa...

🌍 Maps ✅
25-acre common in city centre where Cambridge Rules of football were established 1848 - precursor to modern association football. Large flat green space used for recreation, events, and sports. Reality Checkpoint lamppost marks traditional centre. Free public space. Popular with students and locals for football, frisbee, sunbathing. Hosts bonfire night celebrations and open-air cinema. Surrounded by Victorian terraced houses. Good shortcut across city.

Cambridge University Botanic Garden

40-Acre Garden with 8,000 Plant Species

40-acre garden opened 1846 containing over 8,000 plant species. Glasshouses feature tropical rainforest, alpine plants, and carnivorous plants. System...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
40-acre garden opened 1846 containing over 8,000 plant species. Glasshouses feature tropical rainforest, alpine plants, and carnivorous plants. Systematic beds show plant family relationships. Autumn colour walk, winter garden, and spring bulbs provide year-round interest. Rock garden and lake. Research garden for Cambridge University. Peaceful escape from city centre. Café and gift shop. Allow 1-2 hours minimum.
