🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Picturesque village where River Windrush flows through centre crossed by low stone bridges. Free to explore. Traditional Cotswold stone buildings line river. Model Village shows Bourton in 1:9 scale. Motor Museum and Birdland within village. Very popular so busy during peak times. Good base for Cotswolds exploration. Cafes, pubs, and gift shops. Allow 2-3 hours. Early morning or evening quieter for photography.

Bibury

England's Most Beautiful Village

Arlington Row cottages (14th century) are most photographed in Cotswolds. William Morris called Bibury "most beautiful village in England." Free to wa...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Arlington Row cottages (14th century) are most photographed in Cotswolds. William Morris called Bibury "most beautiful village in England." Free to walk and photograph. River Coln runs through village. Trout farm operates since 1902. Saxon church of St Mary. Small village easily explored on foot. Very popular with tourists and photographers. Limited parking. Best visited early morning. Allow 1 hour. Nearby villages worth exploring too.

Castle Combe

Chocolate Box Village

Frequently called England's prettiest village. Free to explore. 14th-century market cross, ancient bridge, and honey-stone cottages. Film location for...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Frequently called England's prettiest village. Free to explore. 14th-century market cross, ancient bridge, and honey-stone cottages. Film location for Stardust, Dr Dolittle, and War Horse. No overhead wires or road markings preserve historic appearance. Bybrook stream runs through. Medieval wool trade prosperity evident in architecture. Limited facilities - one pub, one shop. Car park outside village. Very popular so arrive early. Allow 1-2 hours wandering.

Broadway

Showpiece Village with Tower

Elegant village on Cotswold Way with wide tree-lined High Street. Free to explore. Galleries, antique shops, cafes, and restaurants. Broadway Tower (f...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Elegant village on Cotswold Way with wide tree-lined High Street. Free to explore. Galleries, antique shops, cafes, and restaurants. Broadway Tower (folly built 1798) on nearby hill offers panoramic views across 16 counties. Arts and Crafts movement connection - William Morris, Edward Burne-Jones visited. More developed than smaller villages. Good shopping and dining. Broadway Tower separate ticket. Village itself free. Allow 2-3 hours.

Chipping Campden

Medieval Wool Town

Historic market town with arcaded Market Hall (1627). Free to explore. Beautiful High Street lined with honey-stone buildings. Wool trade wealth evide...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Historic market town with arcaded Market Hall (1627). Free to explore. Beautiful High Street lined with honey-stone buildings. Wool trade wealth evident in architecture. St James Church (15th century) called "cathedral of the Cotswolds." Court Barn Museum shows local crafts. Arts and Crafts Guild established here 1902. Independent shops and galleries. Less touristy than some villages. Good walking base. Allow 2 hours minimum.

Cirencester

Capital of the Cotswolds

Largest town in Cotswolds, Roman Corinium founded 75 AD. Free to explore historic streets. Corinium Museum shows Roman mosaics and artifacts. St John ...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Largest town in Cotswolds, Roman Corinium founded 75 AD. Free to explore historic streets. Corinium Museum shows Roman mosaics and artifacts. St John the Baptist Church has magnificent perpendicular tower. Market days Monday and Friday. Independent shops and restaurants. Abbey Grounds park free with Roman amphitheatre remains. Good base with facilities. Less "chocolate box" than villages but authentic working town. Allow half day.

Cotswold Way

102-Mile National Trail

102-mile walking trail from Bath to Chipping Campden. Free public footpath. Follows Cotswold escarpment with stunning views. Can walk short sections o...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
102-mile walking trail from Bath to Chipping Campden. Free public footpath. Follows Cotswold escarpment with stunning views. Can walk short sections or complete trail. Passes through historic towns and villages. Well-signed and maintained. Popular day walk sections include Broadway Tower and Cleeve Hill. Mix of gentle and challenging terrain. Beautiful any season. Spring bluebells and autumn colours spectacular. Good boots essential. Maps and guides available.
📷 Saffron Blaze/ Wikimedia Commons / CC BY-SA 3.0

Blenheim Palace

Churchill's Birthplace - UNESCO Site

Magnificent 18th-century palace, only non-royal residence called palace in England. Built 1705-1722 for 1st Duke of Marlborough. Winston Churchill bor...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Magnificent 18th-century palace, only non-royal residence called palace in England. Built 1705-1722 for 1st Duke of Marlborough. Winston Churchill born here 1874. UNESCO World Heritage Site. State Rooms lavishly decorated. Capability Brown landscaped parkland with lake and gardens. Formal gardens and Churchill exhibition. Extensive estate. Allow full day to explore palace and grounds. One of Britain's greatest houses.
