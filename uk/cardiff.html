Cardiff Castle

Roman Fort, Norman Keep, and Victorian Gothic Fantasy

Built on the site of a 3rd-century Roman fort in the heart of the city centre. Norman Keep sits atop a motte dating to the 11th century, offering pano...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Built on the site of a 3rd-century Roman fort in the heart of the city centre. Norman Keep sits atop a motte dating to the 11th century, offering panoramic city views from the summit. The castle apartments were lavishly transformed in Gothic Revival style by architect William Burges for the 3rd Marquess of Bute in the 19th century. Interior rooms feature vivid colours, intricate carvings, murals, stained glass, and gold leaf throughout. Grounds include the Norman Keep, wartime tunnels used as air-raid shelters in WWII, and an interpretation centre. On clear days, Castell Coch is visible from the top of the Keep. Allow half a day to explore fully.

Castell Coch

Victorian Gothic Fairy-Tale Castle

Perched on a wooded hillside overlooking the village of Tongwynlais, approximately 5 miles north of Cardiff city centre. Although a Norman castle stoo...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Perched on a wooded hillside overlooking the village of Tongwynlais, approximately 5 miles north of Cardiff city centre. Although a Norman castle stood on the site originally, the current structure is a 19th-century creation by architect William Burges for the 3rd Marquess of Bute, the same partnership responsible for Cardiff Castle's interiors. Circular towers topped with conical roofs give the appearance of a fairy-tale castle. Interior rooms feature elaborate painted decorations, gilded ceilings, and carved stonework. Managed by Cadw, the Welsh Government's historic environment service. Surrounded by beech woodland with walking trails.

Llandaff Cathedral

Medieval Cathedral in Historic Village Setting

Founded in the 6th century on a site associated with Saints Teilo and Dyfrig, making it one of the oldest Christian sites in Britain. The present buil...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Founded in the 6th century on a site associated with Saints Teilo and Dyfrig, making it one of the oldest Christian sites in Britain. The present building dates primarily from the 12th to 14th centuries with significant Victorian restoration. Jacob Epstein's aluminium sculpture "Christ in Majesty" dominates the nave, installed in 1957. The cathedral was severely damaged by a German bomb in 1941 and rebuilt over subsequent decades. Located in the historic village of Llandaff, which retains a distinct character despite being within Cardiff's boundaries. Pre-Raphaelite artwork by Rossetti and Burne-Jones among the treasures. Free to visit.

National Museum Cardiff

Art, Natural History, and Archaeology Under One Roof

Located in the grand civic centre next to City Hall. Collections span natural history, archaeology, and fine art. Houses one of Europe's finest collec...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Located in the grand civic centre next to City Hall. Collections span natural history, archaeology, and fine art. Houses one of Europe's finest collections of Impressionist paintings including works by Monet, Renoir, Cézanne, and Van Gogh, assembled by the Davies sisters of Gregynog. Natural history galleries feature dinosaur skeletons, the Big Bang, and the evolution of Wales. Archaeology displays cover Welsh history from prehistoric times. The building itself is an imposing Portland stone structure in the Cathays Park civic centre. Part of Amgueddfa Cymru (National Museum Wales). Allow 2-3 hours minimum.

St Fagans National Museum of History

Europe's Leading Open-Air Museum

Free Admission

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
One of Europe's leading open-air museums set across 100 acres on the grounds of St Fagans Castle, a 16th-century Elizabethan manor house. Over 40 original buildings from across Wales have been dismantled, transported here, and rebuilt stone by stone — including farmhouses, a medieval church, a Victorian school, a tannery, miners' cottages, and a working bakery. The collection spans 500 years of Welsh domestic and working life. Costumed interpreters demonstrate traditional crafts including wool spinning, blacksmithing, and timber framing. St Fagans Castle itself houses period interiors and formal gardens. One of the most visited heritage attractions in Wales with extensive indoor galleries covering Welsh history and culture.

Principality Stadium

Wales' National Rugby Stadium - Retractable Roof

Opened in 1999 as the Millennium Stadium, replacing the historic Cardiff Arms Park as the home of Welsh rugby. One of the largest stadiums in Britain ...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Opened in 1999 as the Millennium Stadium, replacing the historic Cardiff Arms Park as the home of Welsh rugby. One of the largest stadiums in Britain with a 74,500 capacity and one of only a few with a fully retractable roof. Located in the city centre directly beside the River Taff, an unusual position for a major stadium. Hosts Welsh rugby internationals, FA Cup finals (when Wembley was being rebuilt), concerts, and major sporting events. Stadium tours take visitors through the players' tunnel, dressing rooms, Royal Box, and pitch-side. The stadium dominates the Cardiff skyline from multiple viewpoints.

Wales Millennium Centre

National Arts Centre for Wales

Opened 2004 in Cardiff Bay as the national arts centre for Wales. Designed by Welsh architect Jonathan Adams, the striking exterior is clad in Welsh b...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Opened 2004 in Cardiff Bay as the national arts centre for Wales. Designed by Welsh architect Jonathan Adams, the striking exterior is clad in Welsh building materials including different slates, wood, and stone, topped with a bronze oxide-tinted stainless steel shell. The façade features an inscription in Welsh and English. Home to Welsh National Opera, BBC National Orchestra of Wales, and other national arts organisations. Hosts West End touring musicals, opera, ballet, contemporary dance, and comedy. Ground floor houses a box office, shops, bar, and brasserie. The building dominates the Cardiff Bay waterfront. Guided backstage tours available.

Senedd Cymru / Welsh Parliament

Sustainable Parliamentary Building on Cardiff Bay

Designed by Richard Rogers Partnership and opened in 2006. The Senedd building houses the debating chamber (Siambr) of the Welsh Parliament. Built wit...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Designed by Richard Rogers Partnership and opened in 2006. The Senedd building houses the debating chamber (Siambr) of the Welsh Parliament. Built with sustainability as a core principle, featuring a distinctive undulating wooden ceiling, a large glass frontage facing Cardiff Bay, and a funnel in the roof that channels wind to power the ventilation system. Visitors can watch debates from the public gallery and take guided tours (booking in advance required). The building is designed as an accessible, transparent parliament for the people of Wales. Located on the waterfront alongside the Wales Millennium Centre. Free to visit.

Cardiff Bay

Europe's Largest Waterfront Development

Regenerated waterfront area created by damming Cardiff Bay in 1999. Freshwater lake surrounded by modern architecture including Wales Millennium Centr...

🌍 Maps ✅
Regenerated waterfront district created by the construction of the Cardiff Bay Barrage in 1999, which impounded the tidal mudflats of the Rivers Taff and Ely to form a freshwater lake. The area houses the Senedd — the Welsh Parliament building designed by Richard Rogers with a distinctive undulating roof — alongside the Wales Millennium Centre performing arts venue, the Norwegian Church Arts Centre where Roald Dahl was christened, and the Pierhead Building's Victorian Gothic clock tower. The Barrage itself provides a walkable causeway stretching over a kilometre with views across the Bristol Channel. Restaurants and bars line Mermaid Quay. Water bus services connect the Bay to the city centre along the river.

Bute Park

City Centre Green Space Along the River Taff

56 hectares of parkland extending from Cardiff Castle along the River Taff towards Llandaff. Originally part of the castle grounds belonging to the Bu...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
56 hectares of parkland extending from Cardiff Castle along the River Taff towards Llandaff. Originally part of the castle grounds belonging to the Bute family. Contains over 3,000 trees including arboretum specimens, a sculpture trail with 21 wooden carvings, and formal gardens. The Blackfriars friary ruins, dating from the 13th century, are visible within the park. Summerhouse Café provides refreshments. The Taff Trail running and cycling path passes through the park connecting Cardiff Bay to Brecon. One of the largest urban parks in Wales. Free to visit.

Norwegian Church Arts Centre

Historic Waterfront Church and Gallery

White clapboard church originally built in 1868 to serve Norwegian sailors who docked at Cardiff's busy coal port. Roald Dahl was baptised here in 191...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
White clapboard church originally built in 1868 to serve Norwegian sailors who docked at Cardiff's busy coal port. Roald Dahl was baptised here in 1916. The church fell into disuse and was dismantled, then reconstructed on its current Cardiff Bay waterfront site in 1992. Now functions as an arts centre hosting art exhibitions, live music, comedy, and community events. The café offers views across Cardiff Bay. A reminder of Cardiff's international maritime history when the docks were among the busiest in the world. Free to enter the gallery and café.

Cardiff Market

Victorian Indoor Market Since 1891

Victorian covered market built in 1891 on a site where markets have operated since medieval times. Two floors of stalls housed within an attractive Vi...

🌍 Maps ✅
Victorian covered market built in 1891 on a site where markets have operated since medieval times. Two floors of stalls housed within an attractive Victorian building with a glass and iron roof. Traditional butchers, fishmongers, bakers, and greengrocers alongside street food stalls, vintage shops, and independent traders. Bakestones sells traditional Welsh cakes cooked fresh on a griddle. The market has become a hub for pop-up street food vendors in recent years. Located in the city centre between St Mary Street and Trinity Street. A working market popular with locals and visitors. Free to browse.
