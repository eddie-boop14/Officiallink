🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Legendary music venue where Beatles performed 292 times between 1961-1963. Current club rebuilt on original site using original bricks after demolition in 1973. Live music daily - tribute bands and original acts. Brick wall signed by Paul McCartney. Intimate basement venue maintains 1960s atmosphere. Beatles performed first gig here March 1961. Mathew Street location now Liverpool's music quarter. Free entry daytime, ticketed evening shows.

Victorian Dock Complex and Museums

Grade I listed Victorian dock complex opened 1846. Free to walk around docks and waterfront. Houses multiple museums including Beatles Story, Tate Liv...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Grade I listed Victorian dock complex opened 1846. Free to walk around docks and waterfront. Houses multiple museums including Beatles Story, Tate Liverpool, Merseyside Maritime Museum, and International Slavery Museum. Restaurants, bars, and shops in converted warehouses. Beautiful cast-iron and brick architecture. Important trading port during British Empire. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Popular for waterfront walks and photography. Allow 3-4 hours to explore complex.

Tate Liverpool

Modern and Contemporary Art Gallery

Free admission

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Branch of the Tate gallery network housed in a converted warehouse on Albert Dock, opened in 1988 as the first Tate gallery outside London. The collection draws on Tate's national holdings of modern and contemporary art with rotating displays that change regularly, complemented by major temporary exhibitions. The converted warehouse space — designed by James Stirling — preserves original cast-iron columns and brick arches while creating flexible gallery spaces across four floors. The gallery played a key role in Liverpool's cultural regeneration alongside the neighbouring Maritime Museum and International Slavery Museum on the same dock. Programming includes artist commissions, educational workshops, and events connecting contemporary art with Liverpool's creative community.

Liverpool Cathedral

Britain's Largest Cathedral

Largest cathedral in Britain and fifth-largest in world. Completed 1978 after 74 years construction. Gothic Revival masterpiece designed by Giles Gilb...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Largest cathedral in Britain and fifth-largest in world. Completed 1978 after 74 years construction. Gothic Revival masterpiece designed by Giles Gilbert Scott at age 22. Tower offers panoramic city and river views - highest point accessible in Liverpool. Great Space interior seats 3,000. World's highest and heaviest ringing bells. Organ has 10,000 pipes. Film and exhibition space in basement. Cathedral visit free, tower ticketed. Active place of worship. Allow 1-2 hours.

Liverpool Metropolitan Cathedral

Modernist Circular Cathedral

Striking modernist Roman Catholic cathedral completed 1967. Circular design with central altar seats 2,000. Nicknamed "Paddy's Wigwam" by locals for c...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Striking modernist Roman Catholic cathedral completed 1967. Circular design with central altar seats 2,000. Nicknamed "Paddy's Wigwam" by locals for conical shape. Lantern tower features stunning stained glass by John Piper and Patrick Reyntiens - largest area of colored glass in world. Designed by Frederick Gibberd. Crypt from earlier unfinished Lutyens design visible below. Free entry. Contrasts dramatically with Gothic Anglican cathedral. Both cathedrals connected by Hope Street. Allow 30-60 minutes.

Sefton Park

235-Acre Victorian Park with Palm House

🌍 Maps ✅
235-acre Victorian park opened 1872. Free entry. Features lake, waterfalls, statues, and walking paths. Palm House (1896) is grade II listed glasshouse containing tropical plants. Park hosts events and festivals. Boating lake, café, and children's play areas. Popular for walking, jogging, and picnics. Beautiful autumn colors. Less touristy than city centre attractions. Good escape from urban environment. Allow 1-2 hours for relaxed visit.

UNESCO World Heritage waterfront featuring Three Graces - Royal Liver Building, Cunard Building, and Port of Liverpool Building. Free to walk waterfront.
