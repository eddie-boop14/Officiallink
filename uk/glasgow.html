Kelvingrove Art Gallery and Museum

Scotland's Most Visited Museum

Free Admission

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Glasgow's most visited museum housed in a magnificent red sandstone building opened in 1901 for the Glasgow International Exhibition. The collection spans art, natural history, and arms and armour across 22 themed galleries. Highlights include Salvador Dalí's Christ of Saint John of the Cross, works by the Glasgow Boys and Scottish Colourists, a Spitfire aircraft suspended from the ceiling, and Charles Rennie Mackintosh furniture and designs. The building itself — Spanish Baroque in style — sits in Kelvingrove Park beside the River Kelvin with views to the University of Glasgow's Gothic tower. Daily organ recitals on the grand Lewis organ fill the central hall. One of the finest civic art collections in Europe with over 8,000 objects on display.

Riverside Museum

Transport and Technology Museum

Free Entry

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Scotland's museum of transport and travel designed by Zaha Hadid, opened in 2011 on the north bank of the River Clyde at the site of the former Inglis shipyard. The striking zinc-clad building features a distinctive zigzag roofline evoking waves. Inside, over 3,000 objects tell Glasgow's story through transport — vintage cars, locomotives, horse-drawn carriages, bicycles, prams, skateboards, and ship models spanning centuries of Clyde-built maritime heritage. A recreated 1930s Glasgow street with period shopfronts runs through the museum. The Tall Ship Glenlee, one of only five Clyde-built sailing ships still afloat, is berthed alongside the museum on the river. Winner of the European Museum of the Year Award in 2013.

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Charles Rennie Mackintosh's first public building (1895), originally Glasgow Herald offices. Now Scotland's centre for design and architecture. Mackintosh Interpretation Centre tells his story. Climb 134 steps to viewing platform for 360-degree Glasgow views. Rotating design and architecture exhibitions. Beautiful spiral staircase. Café and design shop. Allow 1-2 hours. Must-visit for Mackintosh enthusiasts. Central location.

Restored Tearooms

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Fully restored Mackintosh tearoom on Sauchiehall Street. Original 1903 design for Kate Cranston. Every detail designed by Mackintosh including furniture, cutlery, and light fittings. Working tearoom serving afternoon tea. Visitor centre explains restoration and Mackintosh's vision. Room de Luxe especially stunning. Faithful recreation of original scheme. Afternoon tea booking recommended. Exhibition space included with admission. Allow 60-90 minutes. Authentic Mackintosh experience.

Glasgow Cathedral

Medieval Cathedral - St Mungo's Burial Place

Only medieval cathedral on Scottish mainland to survive Reformation intact. Built 12th-15th centuries on site where St Mungo established church 6th ce...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Only medieval cathedral on Scottish mainland to survive Reformation intact. Built 12th-15th centuries on site where St Mungo established church 6th century. Lower church houses St Mungo's tomb. Stunning medieval stained glass. Beautiful stone carvings and vaulted ceiling. Active place of worship. Necropolis Victorian cemetery on hill behind cathedral worth visiting. Allow 1 hour for cathedral and Necropolis together. Atmospheric and historically significant.

Necropolis

Victorian Garden Cemetery

Victorian garden cemetery opened 1832 on hill overlooking cathedral. Free to explore. Over 50,000 burials and 3,500 monuments. Notable Glaswegians bur...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Victorian garden cemetery opened 1832 on hill overlooking cathedral. Free to explore. Over 50,000 burials and 3,500 monuments. Notable Glaswegians buried here including industrialists, architects, and reformers. John Knox statue tops highest point. Spectacular views across Glasgow. Beautiful architecture of tombs and mausoleums. Popular walking spot. Atmospheric and peaceful. Paths can be steep. Allow 45-60 minutes. Combines well with cathedral visit.

George Square

Victorian City Centre Square

Principal civic square surrounded by Victorian architecture. Free public space. City Chambers (1888) dominates east side - beautiful example of Victor...

🌍 Maps ✅
Principal civic square surrounded by Victorian architecture. Free public space. City Chambers (1888) dominates east side - beautiful example of Victorian civic pride. Statues include Robert Burns, James Watt, and Sir Walter Scott on central column. Christmas markets and events held here. Heart of Glasgow's grid system. Good starting point for exploring city. Popular meeting place. Allow 15-30 minutes viewing square and architecture.

Kelvingrove Park

Victorian Park Along River Kelvin

85-acre Victorian park opened 1852. Free entry. Beautiful landscaped gardens along River Kelvin. Kelvingrove Art Gallery and Museum at eastern edge. W...

🌍 Maps ✅
85-acre Victorian park opened 1852. Free entry. Beautiful landscaped gardens along River Kelvin. Kelvingrove Art Gallery and Museum at eastern edge. Walking and cycling paths. Popular for picnics, jogging, and dog walking. Skate park and play areas. Stewart Memorial Fountain centerpiece. Connects to West End via Kelvin Walkway. Peaceful green space close to city center. Allow 1 hour for leisurely walk. Good in all seasons.

Glasgow Green

Historic Common - People's Palace

Ancient common ground dating back over 600 years. Free public park. People's Palace museum tells Glasgow's social history. Winter Gardens glasshouse a...

🌍 Maps ✅
Ancient common ground dating back over 600 years. Free public park. People's Palace museum tells Glasgow's social history. Winter Gardens glasshouse attached to palace. Doulton Fountain largest terracotta fountain in world. McLennan Arch relocated from demolished buildings. Popular for walking, events, and festivals. River Clyde runs alongside. Allow 1-2 hours to explore park and People's Palace. Historic significance as Glasgow's oldest park.

The SSE Hydro

Major Concert and Events Arena

Major entertainment venue opened 2013 at Scottish Event Campus. Capacity 13,000. Hosts concerts, sporting events, and comedy shows. Distinctive shell-...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Major entertainment venue opened 2013 at Scottish Event Campus. Capacity 13,000. Hosts concerts, sporting events, and comedy shows. Distinctive shell-shaped architecture on River Clyde. Major international acts perform here. Second-busiest music arena in world by ticket sales. Modern facilities and excellent acoustics. Part of larger campus including SEC Centre and Armadillo. Check event schedule for performances. Advance booking recommended for popular shows.
