Edinburgh Castle

Dominating Fortress on Castle Rock — Crown Jewels, Stone of Destiny

Perched on an extinct volcano 130 metres above the city, Edinburgh Castle has been a royal residence and military garrison for over 900 years. The Cro...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Perched on an extinct volcano 130 metres above the city, Edinburgh Castle has been a royal residence and military garrison for over 900 years. The Crown Room houses the Honours of Scotland (the Scottish Crown Jewels) and the Stone of Destiny, used in coronations of Scottish and British monarchs. The One O'Clock Gun is fired daily (except Sundays). The uphill walk from the Royal Mile takes about 10 minutes.

Palace of Holyroodhouse

Official Royal Residence — Royal Mile's Eastern End

Official Scottish residence of the British monarch, located at foot of Royal Mile opposite Edinburgh Castle. State Apartments feature historic tapestr...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Official Scottish residence of the British monarch, located at foot of Royal Mile opposite Edinburgh Castle. State Apartments feature historic tapestries and paintings from the Royal Collection. Mary Queen of Scots' chambers preserved, including the room where her secretary Rizzio was murdered in 1566. Ruined Holyrood Abbey adjoins the palace. Beautiful palace gardens open during summer. Palace may close at short notice during royal visits. Audio guide included.

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Served the Royal Family from 1953 to 1997, travelling over one million miles on 968 official voyages. Preserved exactly as during final years of service. St Giles' Cathedral

Medieval Cathedral — Heart of Royal Mile

High Kirk of Edinburgh dating from 14th century with distinctive crown spire landmark. Thistle Chapel (1911) houses the Order of the Thistle, Scotland...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
High Kirk of Edinburgh dating from 14th century with distinctive crown spire landmark. Thistle Chapel (1911) houses the Order of the Thistle, Scotland's highest chivalric order. John Knox preached here during the Reformation - his statue stands inside. Beautiful stained glass windows including the Burne-Jones window. Free entry with donations requested. Tower tours available for small fee offering city views. Active place of worship with daily services.

Real Mary King's Close

Sealed 17th-Century Underground Streets

Network of closes (narrow alleys) sealed beneath the Royal Mile when City Chambers built above in 18th century. Preserved 17th-century streets show li...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Network of closes (narrow alleys) sealed beneath the Royal Mile when City Chambers built above in 18th century. Preserved 17th-century streets show life during plague years and poverty. Guided tours only, lasting 75 minutes with costumed character guides. Tours cover plague, executions, and daily life in Old Edinburgh. Involves stairs and uneven surfaces. Temperature around 10°C year-round. Advance booking recommended.

Rosslyn Chapel

15th-Century Chapel Famous for Intricate Carvings

Founded 1446, famous for extraordinarily intricate stone carvings covering walls and ceiling. The Apprentice Pillar is the chapel's masterpiece. Featu...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Founded 1446, famous for extraordinarily intricate stone carvings covering walls and ceiling. The Apprentice Pillar is the chapel's masterpiece. Featured in Da Vinci Code novel and film. Small but incredibly detailed - every surface carved. Guidebook recommended to understand symbolism. Located in village of Roslin, 7 miles south of Edinburgh. Bus 37 from city centre takes 30 minutes. Active place of worship. Photography not permitted inside. Allow 1-2 hours including visitor centre.

Free admission to vast collection spanning Scottish history, natural history, science, and world cultures. Highlights include Dolly the Sheep (first c...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Free admission to vast collection spanning Scottish history, natural history, science, and world cultures. Highlights include Dolly the Sheep (first cloned mammal), Lewis Chessmen, and St Kilda mailboat. Scottish galleries cover Pictish stones through to 20th century. Rooftop terrace on Level 7 offers panoramic city views (free access). Grand Gallery features soaring Victorian architecture. Special exhibitions may charge. Café and restaurant on site. Allow minimum 2-3 hours, could easily spend full day.

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Free admission to exceptional collection in neoclassical building on the Mound. Works from 14th to early 20th century including Botticelli, Titian, Rembrandt, Velázquez, Turner, Monet, and Van Gogh. Scottish paintings feature prominently. Two connected buildings linked by underground passage. Compact galleries can be seen thoroughly in 1-2 hours. Free guided tours available. Special exhibitions may charge. Located between Old Town and New Town with Princes Street Gardens views.

Royal Mile

Medieval Street Connecting Castle to Palace

Historic mile-long street descending from Edinburgh Castle to Holyrood Palace through Old Town. Actually comprises four streets: Castlehill, Lawnmarke...

🌍 Maps ✅
Historic mile-long street descending from Edinburgh Castle to Holyrood Palace through Old Town. Actually comprises four streets: Castlehill, Lawnmarket, High Street, and Canongate. Narrow closes (alleys) lead off main street to hidden courtyards. St Giles' Cathedral marks the midpoint. Lined with historic buildings, pubs, restaurants, and shops. Street performers and bagpipers common. Free to walk. Allow 1-2 hours to explore properly. Very busy during August festivals. Cobblestones can be slippery when wet.

Grassmarket

Historic Market Square Below Castle Rock

Historic square beneath Edinburgh Castle used for markets since 1477. Once site of public executions - last held 1784. Now filled with pubs, restauran...

🌍 Maps ✅
Historic square beneath Edinburgh Castle used for markets since 1477. Once site of public executions - last held 1784. Now filled with pubs, restaurants, independent shops, and vintage stores. Stunning castle views from every angle. Popular with locals and tourists. Friday and Saturday markets sell crafts and street food. Lively nightlife area. Victoria Street (inspiration for Diagon Alley in Harry Potter) leads uphill to Royal Mile. Free to explore.

Arthur's Seat

Extinct Volcano — Edinburgh's Highest Point

Extinct volcano rising 251 metres in Holyrood Park, dominating Edinburgh's skyline. Main summit path takes 45-60 minutes from palace. Spectacular 360-...

🌍 Maps ✅
Extinct volcano rising 251 metres in Holyrood Park, dominating Edinburgh's skyline. Main summit path takes 45-60 minutes from palace. Spectacular 360-degree views across Edinburgh, Firth of Forth, and surrounding hills. Multiple routes from easy to challenging. Best approached from Holyrood Palace or Dunsapie Loch. Wear proper footwear - rocky and steep in places. Can be windy at top. Free access. Sunrise and sunset particularly beautiful. Bring water. Descent takes 30-40 minutes.

Panoramic Viewpoint with Iconic Monuments

Hilltop offering spectacular panoramic views, especially at sunset. Features National Monument (unfinished Parthenon replica), Nelson Monument (climb ...

🌍 Maps ✅
Hilltop offering spectacular panoramic views, especially at sunset. Features National Monument (unfinished Parthenon replica), Nelson Monument (climb for views), City Observatory, and Dugald Stewart Monument. Easy 10-minute walk from Princes Street. Free access to hill and monuments (small charge to climb Nelson Monument). Popular spot for photography. Sunset views looking towards castle are iconic Edinburgh images. Less strenuous than Arthur's Seat. Can be windy.

Princes Street Gardens

Victorian Park Dividing Old and New Towns

35-acre park created when Nor Loch was drained in 1820s, separating Old Town from New Town. Divided into East and West gardens by the Mound. Features ...

🌍 Maps ✅
35-acre park created when Nor Loch was drained in 1820s, separating Old Town from New Town. Divided into East and West gardens by the Mound. Features include Scott Monument (climb 287 steps for views), Ross Fountain, Floral Clock, and seasonal flowerbeds. Free entry. Perfect for picnics with castle views. Outdoor concerts and events during festivals. Quieter than city streets. Winter ice rink installed annually. Easy access from Princes Street shopping area.

Greyfriars Kirkyard

Historic Cemetery — Greyfriars Bobby's Burial Ground

Historic 16th-century graveyard containing elaborate memorials and famous graves. Greyfriars Bobby statue outside commemorates loyal terrier who guard...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Historic 16th-century graveyard containing elaborate memorials and famous graves. Greyfriars Bobby statue outside commemorates loyal terrier who guarded owner's grave for 14 years. Covenanters' Prison holds memorial to religious martyrs. Atmospheric setting popular with ghost tours. J.K. Rowling found inspiration for Harry Potter names on gravestones (Tom Riddle, McGonagall). Free access during daylight. Greyfriars Kirk (church) also open for visits. National Covenant signed here 1638. Respectful behavior requested.
