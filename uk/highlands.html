Ben Nevis

Britain's Highest Mountain

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Britain's highest mountain at 1,345 metres. Free to climb. Mountain Track (tourist route) from Glen Nevis most popular. Summit typically takes 7-9 hours return. Weather extremely changeable - full mountain equipment essential even summer. Often cloud-covered - summit views reward clear days. Over 150,000 climb annually. Start early to avoid crowds. Serious undertaking requiring fitness and preparation. Fort William base town. Alternative routes more challenging.

Spectacular glen with dramatic mountain scenery. Free to drive through and walk. Site of 1692 Glencoe Massacre. Three Sisters mountains iconic view. National Trust Visitor Centre tells history. 🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Remote glen west of Loch Ness with ancient Caledonian pine forest. Free access. Beautiful walks from easy lochside to challenging munros. Dog Falls popular short walk. Lochs Affric and Beinn a' Mheadhoin stunning. Scottish Natural Heritage reserve. Wildlife including red deer, pine martens, golden eagles. Limited facilities - bring provisions. Single track road. Less visited than Glencoe but equally beautiful. Allow full day. True wilderness feel.

Urquhart Castle

Loch Ness Castle Ruins

Medieval castle ruins on Loch Ness shores. 1,000 years of history from Picts through Jacobites. Grant Tower offers views across loch. Visitor centre s...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🌐 Official Site ✅
Ruined medieval fortress on a rocky promontory jutting into Loch Ness, offering some of the most photographed views in Scotland. The castle's strategic position controlling the Great Glen made it a prize fought over for centuries — it was held and lost by Edward I of England, Robert the Bruce, and various Highland clans before being deliberately blown up in 1692 to prevent Jacobite forces using it. The Grant Tower, partially intact, provides elevated views down the length of Loch Ness in both directions. Visitors descend from the visitor centre to the lakeshore ruins through a modern exhibition explaining the castle's turbulent history. The site is one of the most popular spots for Loch Ness monster watchers — the deep water directly below the castle walls is where many claimed sightings have occurred. Allow one to two hours including the exhibition and castle grounds.

Curved railway viaduct completed 1901. Free viewpoint access. Jacobite steam train crosses twice daily summer (separate ticket). Featured as Hogwarts Express route in Harry Potter films. 🌍 Maps ✅
Highland capital at north end of Great Glen. Free to explore city centre. Inverness Castle overlooks River Ness (not open to public but good viewpoint). Victorian Market houses independent shops. Cathedral and riverside walks pleasant. Good base for Loch Ness, Culloden, and northern Highlands. Dolphin watching cruises from harbor. Shopping and restaurants. Transport hub for region. Allow half day city exploration.

Loch Ness

Famous Loch and Monster Legend

Britain's largest body of freshwater by volume, 23 miles long. Free to drive alongside. Nessie monster legend attracts tourists. Loch Ness Centre in D...

🌍 Maps ✅
Britain's largest body of freshwater by volume, 23 miles long. Free to drive alongside. Nessie monster legend attracts tourists. Loch Ness Centre in Drumnadrochit tells monster story. Boat cruises available multiple locations. Great Glen Way walking route follows lochside. Beautiful scenery. Swimming brave - water stays cold year-round. Monster sightings unlikely but loch beautiful regardless. Allow day to drive round and explore.
