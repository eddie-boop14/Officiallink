Malham Cove

Curved Limestone Cliff

80-metre high curved limestone cliff formed by glacial meltwater. Free access. Walk to top via steps for stunning views across dales. Limestone paveme...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Curved limestone cliff 80 metres high and 300 metres wide, formed at the end of the last Ice Age by a waterfall that once rivalled Niagara in scale. The limestone pavement at the top — eroded into deep fissures called grykes separating flat slabs called clints — supports rare ferns, flowers, and wildlife found nowhere else in England. The pavement featured as a filming location in Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows. A well-maintained path climbs 400 stone steps from Malham village to the top. The cove sits within the Yorkshire Dales National Park and can be combined with walks to nearby Gordale Scar and Malham Tarn. Free to visit. Allow two to three hours for the circular walk from Malham village including the pavement.

Aysgarth Falls

Series of Waterfalls on River Ure

Three-tier waterfall in Wensleydale. Free access with parking charge. Upper, Middle, and Lower Falls each beautiful. Woodland walks connect falls. Mos...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Series of three broad limestone waterfalls — Upper, Middle, and Lower Falls — where the River Ure drops through a wooded gorge in Wensleydale. The falls spread across the full width of the river, creating a dramatic curtain effect especially after heavy rainfall. Ancient woodland lines both banks with a network of paths connecting all three falls. The Middle Falls featured in the film Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves. The Yorkshire Museum of Carriages and Horse-Drawn Vehicles sits beside the falls. A circular walk of approximately two miles links all three falls through oak and ash woodland. Free to access the falls on foot. Parking available at the National Park Centre. One of the most visited natural attractions in the Yorkshire Dales.

🌍 Maps ✅
Challenge walk covering three peaks in 24 hours. Free but demanding. Total 24 miles with 5,000 feet ascent. Pen-y-ghent (694m), Whernside (736m), and Ingleborough (723m). Popular charity challenge. Requires good fitness and preparation. Weather can change rapidly. Most start Horton in Ribblesdale. Each peak can be climbed separately. Beautiful scenery throughout. Allow 10-12 hours for circuit. Not suitable for inexperienced walkers.

Fountains Abbey

Largest Abbey Ruins in England

Magnificent Cistercian abbey ruins founded 1132. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Set in Studley Royal Water Garden - Georgian landscape garden. Abbey chur...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Magnificent Cistercian abbey ruins founded 1132. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Set in Studley Royal Water Garden - Georgian landscape garden. Abbey church remains spectacular. Cellarium remarkably intact. Beautiful riverside setting. Deer park and medieval mill. National Trust property. Allow 3-4 hours to explore abbey and gardens properly. One of Britain's finest abbey ruins. Pre-booking essential.

Robin Hood's Bay

Clifftop Fishing Village

Picturesque fishing village tumbling down cliff to sea. Free to explore. Narrow streets and alleyways descend steeply. Former smuggling village. Fossi...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Former smuggling village clinging to steep cliffs on the North Yorkshire coast, connected by narrow alleys, hidden passages, and steep stone steps. Legend holds that contraband could be passed from the dock to the top of the village without seeing daylight. The lower village — a jumble of red-roofed cottages compressed between the cliff face and the sea — is accessible only on foot. The beach at low tide reveals rock pools, fossils, and the remains of a petrified forest. Robin Hood's Bay marks the eastern end of the Coast to Coast Walk from St Bees in Cumbria. The village has no connection to Robin Hood — the name's origin is disputed. Parking is at the top of the village with a steep walk down. Fossil hunting along the foreshore is popular at low tide.

Harrogate

Elegant Spa Town

Victorian spa town known for elegant architecture and gardens. Free to explore. Valley Gardens beautiful public park. Turkish Baths still operate (tic...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Elegant spa town in North Yorkshire known for its Victorian architecture, gardens, and tea rooms. The Royal Pump Room Museum occupies the former sulphur well building where visitors came to take the mineral waters — the strongest sulphur spring in Europe. Valley Gardens cover 17 acres of formal flowerbeds, woodland, and the Sun Colonnade. Betty's Tea Rooms, established in 1919 by a Swiss confectioner, is an institution serving afternoon tea in an art deco dining room. The Stray — 200 acres of protected grassland — surrounds the town centre, preserved by Act of Parliament. The Turkish Baths on Parliament Street, restored to their Victorian splendour, offer the most complete surviving example of a Turkish bath in Britain. Harrogate hosts the Great Yorkshire Show and the Harrogate Flower Show.
