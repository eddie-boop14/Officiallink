St Michael's Mount

Tidal Island Castle

Medieval castle and chapel on tidal island reached by causeway at low tide. National Trust property. Boat ferries operate at high tide. Castle shows f...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Tidal island crowned by a medieval castle and church, accessible on foot across a granite causeway at low tide or by boat when the tide is in. The castle dates from the 12th century and was originally a Benedictine priory linked to Mont Saint-Michel in Normandy. Interior rooms include the Chevy Chase Hall with its 15th-century plaster hunting frieze, the Blue Drawing Room with Rococo Gothic decoration, and the castle church at the summit. Subtropical gardens terraced into the steep granite slopes feature plants from around the world that thrive in the mild Gulf Stream climate. The island has been home to the St Aubyn family since 1659. Allow two to three hours including the causeway crossing and castle tour. Tide times determine access — check before visiting.

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Dramatic clifftop ruins linked to King Arthur legend. Medieval castle remains on wild headland. New footbridge spans gorge linking mainland to island. Spectacular coastal views. Archaeological finds show site occupied from Roman times through medieval period. Merlin's Cave below accessible at low tide. Steep climbs required. Pre-booking recommended busy periods. Allow 2 hours. Atmospheric and beautiful.

England's most southwesterly point. Free to walk headland and coastal paths. Famous signpost shows distances to worldwide locations. Visitor attractions on site separately ticketed. Dramatic granite cliffs and crashing waves. Good walking along coast path both directions. Popular starting/ending point for Land's End to John O'Groats journeys. Can be windy. Parking charges apply. Coastal scenery spectacular. Allow 1-2 hours.

Eden Project

Giant Biomes with World's Plants

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Massive biomes in former clay pit containing plants from tropical and Mediterranean climates. Opened 2001. Rainforest Biome largest greenhouse in world. Mediterranean Biome shows drought-adapted plants. Outdoor gardens show temperate climate plants. Educational exhibits about environment and sustainability. Events and concerts throughout year. Allow 3-4 hours minimum. Pre-booking essential. One of Cornwall's top attractions. Near St Austell.

Lost Gardens of Heligan

Victorian Gardens Restored

200-acre estate with Victorian gardens lost for decades, restored 1990s. Productive gardens, jungle area, woodland walks, and wildlife project. Kitche...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅🎟 Book Official ✅
Restored Victorian pleasure gardens covering 200 acres near Mevagissey, rediscovered in 1990 after decades of neglect following the First World War when the estate's gardeners were killed in the trenches. The restoration — led by Tim Smit before he created the Eden Project — uncovered a remarkable collection of productive gardens, subtropical jungle valley, Italian garden, and pleasure grounds buried beneath decades of bramble and fallen trees. The Jungle Valley descends through tree ferns, bamboo, and giant rhubarb to ponds fed by natural springs. The productive gardens supply heritage vegetable varieties and fruit from restored Victorian glasshouses. Allow three to four hours. One of the most celebrated garden restoration projects in Europe.

St Ives

Artists' Town and Beaches

Picturesque fishing town famous for artists and beaches. Free to explore narrow streets and harbor. Tate St Ives shows modern and contemporary art ove...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Picturesque fishing town famous for artists and beaches. Free to explore narrow streets and harbor. Tate St Ives shows modern and contemporary art overlooking Porthmeor Beach. Barbara Hepworth Museum and Sculpture Garden in town centre. Four beaches including Porthminster and Carbis Bay. Art galleries throughout town. Gets extremely busy summer. Park and ride recommended. Beautiful light attracted artists for generations. Allow full day.

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
Working fishing port on Camel Estuary. Free to explore harbor and old town. Rick Stein's restaurants made Padstow famous for food. Traditional pubs and fish restaurants. Camel Trail cycling path starts here - 18 miles to Bodmin. Good beaches nearby including Trevone and Constantine. May Day 'Obby 'Oss festival ancient tradition. Popular sailing and watersports. Parking difficult peak season. Allow half day minimum.

Kynance Cove

Turquoise Waters and Serpentine Rock

One of Cornwall's most beautiful beaches with turquoise water and white sand. National Trust site. Free beach access, parking charges apply. Unique se...

🌐 Official Site ✅🌍 Maps ✅
One of Cornwall's most beautiful beaches with turquoise water and white sand. National Trust site. Free beach access, parking charges apply. Unique serpentine rock formations. Accessible at low tide. Rock pools good for children. Café at top of cliff. Lizard Peninsula location. South West Coast Path passes through. Popular but worth visiting. Check tide times. Allow 2-3 hours. Beautiful for photography.
