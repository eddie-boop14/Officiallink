About

The Museo Fallero de València (Fallas Museum) is a municipal museum housed in a historic building at Plaza Monteolivete, 4, near the Turia Gardens in Valencia. Its permanent collection consists primarily of ninots indultats — the figurines that, each year since 1934, have been spared from the traditional burning (cremà) of the Las Fallas festival by popular vote. The collection spans nearly a century of fallas craftsmanship, from early figures with wax heads and real clothing to modern papier-mâché and polystyrene sculptures.

In addition to the ninot collection, the museum displays official Fallas festival posters dating back to the early twentieth century, portraits of the Falleres Majors de València (festival queens), and awards and insignia from fallas commissions. An explanatory video on the history and structure of Las Fallas is available inside. The Las Fallas festival was declared Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity by UNESCO in 2016. Audio guides in Spanish, Valencian, English, French and Italian are available for €1 via personal device.

The museum is free every Sunday and on public holidays, and free year-round for visitors under 16. Groups of more than 10 must book in advance by emailing visitesmuseufaller@valencia.es. For direct enquiries, the museum can be reached at +34 963 525 478 or +34 962 084 625. The venue sits close to the old Turia riverbed, making a combined visit with the Turia Gardens and the City of Arts and Sciences practical from a single base.

FAQ
How much does Museo Fallero cost?
Regular admission is €2. Reduced rate (children 7–12, seniors 65+, pensioners, disabled, unemployed) is €1. Entry is free every Sunday and on public holidays, and free year-round for visitors under 16. Under 7s and school groups with accreditation also enter free. Pay at the door — no advance booking is required for individual visitors.
What are the opening hours?
Tuesday to Saturday: 10:00–19:00 (last individual entry 18:00, last group entry 17:00). Sundays and public holidays: 10:00–14:00 (last individual entry 13:00, last group entry 12:30). Closed every Monday and on 1 January, 6 January, 1 May and 25 December.
Where is the Museo Fallero located?
Plaza Monteolivete, 4, Valencia. The museum is located near the dry Turia riverbed, between the Palau de les Arts and the City of Arts and Sciences. City buses 13, 15, 19, 24, 25, 35, 40, 94 and 95 serve the surrounding area.
Is there an audio guide?
Yes. Audio guides cost €1 and are available in 5 languages: Spanish, Valencian, English, French and Italian. They function on your own smartphone or tablet and can be accessed an unlimited number of times on that device.
Can groups visit the Museo Fallero?
Groups of more than 10 people must book in advance by emailing visitesmuseufaller@valencia.es with the group name, city of origin, number of students or visitors, number of accompanying staff, and preferred date and time. No more than 25 people may enter simultaneously. A group reduced rate of €1 per person applies.
