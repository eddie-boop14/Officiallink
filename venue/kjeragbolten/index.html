About

Kjeragbolten is a 5 m³ boulder wedged in a rock crevasse on the Kjerag mountain plateau, 984 metres above Lysefjord. The boulder was deposited in its current position by a glacier approximately 50,000 years ago. Kjerag mountain itself reaches 1,084 metres, making it the highest peak along the Lysefjord. The site is also one of Norway's most active BASE jumping locations, with jumpers launching from the plateau in summer season. The fjord view from the plateau encompasses Lysefjord from Lysebotn to beyond Preikestolen.

The trailhead is at Øygardstøl car park, accessible by a 2.5-hour drive from Stavanger via the Sirdal route, or via the Lysebotn ferry and the 27-hairpin Lysevegen mountain road. The round-trip trail is approximately 11 km with 750–800 m total elevation gain. The route features three steep ascents with fixed chain sections, two valley descents, rocky and exposed terrain, and barren high-altitude landscape. The trail is rated red (demanding) by Norway's hiking scale and is only recommended for experienced hikers in good physical condition.

Stepping onto Kjeragbolten itself is optional and done entirely at personal risk — there are no safety barriers and the boulder surface is curved. The trail is open from approximately late May to October. The access road (Lysevegen) is closed from November to mid-May due to snow and ice. Approximately 70,000 hikers complete the trail annually. The trailhead operates a tourist information office from May to October with daily weather and trail condition updates posted on their Facebook page.

Best time: Weekdays in late May or September offer the lowest crowds. In July–August, sunny weekends see the longest queues at the boulder itself — arriving at the car park by 07:00 allows you to reach Kjeragbolten before the queue builds. Kjerag is approximately 10°C colder than Stavanger — bring warmer layers regardless of weather in the city. Rain makes the granite chain sections dangerous; check conditions at kjerag.info before departure.
FAQ
Do I need a ticket to hike to Kjeragbolten?
No entry ticket is required. The only mandatory cost is parking at Øygardstøl: NOK 300 per car (approximately €27). Payment is by card at automatic barriers. The trail itself is free under Norway's right-to-roam laws. No booking is required to hike.
How long is the Kjeragbolten hike?
The round-trip trail from the Øygardstøl car park is approximately 11 km with 750–800 m elevation gain. The hike takes 5–6 hours for most experienced hikers. The trail features three steep ascents assisted by fixed chains. There are no facilities on the trail itself — only at the trailhead.
When is Kjeragbolten accessible?
The Kjerag parking and trail are open from approximately late May to October. The access road, Lysevegen, is closed from November to mid-May due to snow and ice. Outside the open season, the hike is only accessible with specialist winter equipment and a guide. Check kjerag.info for current trail conditions.
How do I get to Kjeragbolten from Stavanger?
By car: approximately 2.5 hours via the Sirdal route (Lysevegen), or take the ferry to Lysebotn and drive up 27 hairpin bends to Øygardstøl. By bus: Go Fjords runs a seasonal daily bus from Stavanger (Jul–Aug) and weekend service in September, departing at 07:45. Book at gofjords.com.
Is Kjeragbolten suitable for all hikers?
No. The hike is rated red (demanding) on Norway's hiking scale and is recommended for experienced hikers in good physical condition. The trail includes three steep climbs with fixed chain sections and exposed ridgeline sections. It is not recommended for inexperienced hikers. Stepping onto the boulder itself is optional and done entirely at personal risk.
