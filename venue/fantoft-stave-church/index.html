About

Fantoft Stave Church stands in the Fantoft district of Bergen, approximately 4 km south of the city centre. The original structure was built around 1150 in Fortun, Sogn og Fjordane, and was one of several hundred stave churches constructed in Norway during the medieval period. When the congregation at Fortun needed a larger church in 1879, Consul Fredrik Georg Gade purchased the building and had it dismantled and reconstructed on his property at Fantoft, where it served as a family chapel. During relocation, the church was reconstructed to resemble Borgund Stave Church, including the addition of a west tower and dragon-head ridge ornaments.

On 6 June 1992, the church was destroyed by arson. The fire left only the structural framework. The decision to rebuild was made immediately, though the reconstruction presented significant challenges: stave churches had not been built in Norway for several centuries, and most of the original timber was lost. Builders sourced 350–400 year old pine from approximately 400 metres altitude in Kaupanger. The reconstruction was completed and the church was consecrated in August 1997. Two objects from the original church survived: a wish-stone set into one wall, and the cross at the altar. A protective fence was installed around the perimeter after reconstruction.

The church is located at Fantoftvegen 38 in a wooded hillside setting. The interior is open to visitors during the summer season only (approximately May–September); the exterior and grounds can be visited year-round. The church is not accessible for wheelchairs or strollers. Tickets are purchased at the entrance by credit card only; sales close 15 minutes before closing time. Bergen Card holders enter free.

FAQ
How much does Fantoft Stave Church cost to enter?
Adult admission is NOK 80 (~€7.00). Children under 16 enter free. Bergen Card holders enter free. Tickets are purchased at the entrance (credit card only). Ticket sales close 15 minutes before closing time.
When is Fantoft Stave Church open?
The church interior is open seasonally: approximately 2 May – 15 September daily 10:30–18:00, and 16 September – 1 October daily 10:30–16:00. The exterior can be viewed year-round at no charge. Check fantoftstavkirke.no for the current season's exact dates.
How do I get to Fantoft Stave Church from Bergen city centre?
Take Bybanen Line 1 to the Fantoft stop, then walk past the supermarket, turn left at the crossroads and follow signs marked Stave Church — approximately 10 minutes on foot. Alternatively, alight at Paradis stop and take the steeper path up Birkelundsbakken.
Is the stave church the original building?
No. The original church was built c.1150 in Fortun, Sogn and relocated to Fantoft in 1883. It was destroyed by arson on 6 June 1992 and rebuilt using 350–400 year old pine from Kaupanger, completed and consecrated in August 1997. Only a wish-stone in one wall and the altar cross survived from the original structure.
Is Fantoft Stave Church accessible for wheelchairs or strollers?
No. The church is not accessible for wheelchairs or strollers due to the steep path and structure of the building. Toilets are located on the top floor of the adjacent building.
