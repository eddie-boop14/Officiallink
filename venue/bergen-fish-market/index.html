About

Bergen Fish Market, known in Norwegian as Fisketorget, has operated on Bergen's main harbour waterfront since at least the 13th century — first recorded in 1278 near St. Nikolai's Church. In 1558, the market was relocated to its current position along Vågen waterfront on the orders of Christoffer Valkendorf. The market expanded through the 20th century, adding a flower market in 1923 and a fruit and vegetable section. Today it occupies two distinct zones: an outdoor square and the year-round indoor Mathallen hall, which opened in 2012 and houses permanent fish shops and restaurants.

The outdoor market is active from 1 May to 30 September, with stalls selling fresh and cooked seafood — including salmon, shrimp, king crab, fish cakes, and whale meat — alongside fruit, vegetables, and local crafts. Core outdoor hours are 10:00–18:00 in summer. The indoor Mathallen operates throughout the year, Monday–Saturday 09:00–21:30 and Sunday 10:00–21:30, providing a sheltered alternative. Individual vendors inside Mathallen set their own hours. On alternate Saturdays from spring through autumn, the square hosts an expanded farmers' market featuring local cheeses, cured meats, honey, and seasonal produce.

The market is administered by Bergen Municipality and is classified as a public space — there is no admission fee to enter. It is located at Torget, 5014 Bergen, directly on the harbour between the historic Bryggen wharf and the city centre. The market is a few minutes' walk from Fløibanen funicular and the main bus and light-rail interchange at Byparken.

Best time: Weekday mornings before 11:00 for the freshest catches and lightest crowds. Saturday afternoons are at peak volume — the harbour fills with cruise passengers, tour groups, and local shoppers simultaneously. The indoor Mathallen is less crowded than the outdoor square at all times. Avoid the 12:00–14:00 lunchtime rush on any day in July and August.
FAQ
Is Bergen Fish Market free to enter?
Yes. There is no entry fee for the market. Individual food stalls and restaurants inside Mathallen set their own prices for food and seafood purchases. The outdoor square is a public space with no charge at any time.
What are the opening hours of Bergen Fish Market?
Mathallen (indoor hall) is open Monday–Saturday 09:00–21:30 and Sunday 10:00–21:30, year-round, closed 25 December. The outdoor market operates 1 May–30 September with core hours 10:00–18:00. Individual vendors inside may keep different hours.
What can I buy at Bergen Fish Market?
The market sells fresh and cooked seafood including salmon, shrimp, king crab, fish cakes, and whale meat. Fruit, vegetables, and flowers are also available. Multiple restaurants inside Mathallen serve prepared seafood dishes for eating on-site.
Where exactly is Bergen Fish Market?
The market is at Torget, 5014 Bergen — on the main harbour waterfront between Bryggen and the city centre. It is approximately 2 minutes on foot from Fløibanen funicular, 5 minutes from the Byparken light-rail and bus interchange.
What is the official information source for Bergen Fish Market?
The market is administered by Bergen Municipality. The official information page is bergen.kommune.no/hvaskjer/tema/fisketorget. Visit Bergen (en.visitbergen.com) also maintains verified practical visitor information.
