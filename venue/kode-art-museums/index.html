About

KODE is Bergen's primary art and design museum, operating across four connected buildings arranged along Rasmus Meyers allé beside lake Lille Lungegårdsvann in the city centre. The four buildings are Permanenten (decorative arts and design), Lysverket (temporary exhibitions and a Michelin-recognised restaurant), Rasmus Meyer (Norwegian art 1700–1900 and the Edvard Munch collection), and Stenersen (temporary exhibitions). One admission ticket, purchased at kodebergen.no for NOK 200, grants same-day access to all open buildings and also covers entry to Troldhaugen — the Edvard Grieg Museum — on the same day.

The permanent collection spans Norwegian and international art from the 18th century to the present. Key holdings include major works by Edvard Munch, Nikolai Astrup, J.C. Dahl, and Harriet Backer, as well as an extensive collection of decorative arts, silver, and applied design. In addition to the four city-centre buildings, KODE also administers the composers' homes at Troldhaugen (Edvard Grieg), Siljustøl (Harald Sæverud), and the Ole Bull villa at Lysøen (currently closed for renovation). English-language guided tours run daily in summer (June–August); Norwegian tours run every Saturday and Sunday at 13:00 and 14:00.

The four museum buildings are located a short walk from Bergen's main railway station and bus terminal. The closest Bergen Light Rail stops are Byparken and Nonneseter. Car parking is available at GriegGarasjen, Lars Hilles gate 3, approximately 5 minutes on foot. All buildings are wheelchair-accessible via ramp or lift, with reserved parking directly outside Rasmus Meyer. Companion cards are accepted. Bags larger than 30×35×10 cm must be left in the cloakroom.

Best time: Monday or Tuesday morning when the museum opens. Saturday afternoons are the busiest period — guided tours at 13:00 and 14:00 concentrate visitors. The four buildings spread crowds effectively; Stenersen and Permanenten are consistently less visited than Rasmus Meyer. In summer, arrive before 11:00 to avoid cruise-ship group visits.
FAQ
How much does KODE cost?
One ticket costs NOK 200 (approx. €17.00) and gives same-day entry to all open KODE art museum buildings — Permanenten, Lysverket, Rasmus Meyer, and Stenersen — as well as Troldhaugen. Children under 18 enter free. Kode members enter free. Buy at kodebergen.no.
What can I see at KODE?
KODE holds major works by Edvard Munch, Nikolai Astrup, J.C. Dahl, and Harriet Backer, plus international design and craft collections. Rasmus Meyer houses the Munch collection and Norwegian art 1700–1900. Lysverket and Stenersen present temporary exhibitions. Permanenten covers decorative arts and design.
Where are the KODE museums located?
All four buildings line Rasmus Meyers allé along lake Lille Lungegårdsvann. The closest Bergen Light Rail stops are Byparken and Nonneseter. The nearest car park is GriegGarasjen, Lars Hilles gate 3, approximately 5 minutes' walk from the buildings.
Are guided tours available at KODE?
Yes. Norwegian-language guided tours run every Saturday and Sunday at 13:00 and 14:00. English-language guided tours are available daily in summer (June–August). Photography for private use without flash is permitted in all buildings.
Does one KODE ticket cover Troldhaugen too?
Yes. The NOK 200 Kode ticket covers all open art museum buildings in the city centre and also grants entry to Troldhaugen (Edvard Grieg Museum) on the same day. Tickets are available at kodebergen.no.
