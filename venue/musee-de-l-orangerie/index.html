About

Built in 1852 to shelter the orange trees of the Tuileries Garden in winter, the Orangerie was transformed into a museum after World War I at the request of Claude Monet himself. The centrepiece is Les Nymphéas — eight monumental Water Lilies murals painted over 30 years, installed in two purpose-built oval rooms with natural overhead light, exactly as Monet designed. The paintings wrap 360 degrees around the viewer, offering what Monet called an illusion of an endless whole. The rooms were restored to their original skylighted form during a major renovation completed in 2006.

The lower level houses the Walter-Guillaume collection — 144 works assembled by art dealer Paul Guillaume and his wife Domenica, covering the first decades of the 20th century: Renoir, Cézanne, Matisse, Picasso, Modigliani, Soutine, and Derain. Quieter than the Musée d'Orsay and far less crowded. Current temporary exhibition: Henri Rousseau, The Ambition of Painting (March 25–July 20, 2026).

Best time: Thursday or Friday morning — the museum is noticeably quieter after Monday/Wednesday (when tourists compensate for the Tuesday closure). Friday late-night opening until 9pm is also a great option — reduced rate from 6pm and a calmer atmosphere. Arrive at opening (9am) on any day for the best experience with the Water Lilies.
FAQ
How much does Musée de l'Orangerie cost?
Full price is €12.50. Reduced tickets at €9.50 are available for non-EU visitors under 26, students, and disabled visitors. Entry is free for EU citizens under 26, all children under 18, and for all visitors on the first Sunday of each month — though a free timed reservation is still mandatory. On Fridays during exhibition periods the evening rate from 6pm is €10. Book only at billetterie.musee-orangerie.fr.
What are the opening hours and which day is it closed?
Open Monday and Wednesday to Sunday, 9am to 6pm. On Fridays during exhibition periods the museum stays open until 9pm (last entry 8:15pm, galleries close 8:45pm). Closed every Tuesday, plus December 25, May 1, and the morning of July 14. Rooms begin closing 30 minutes before the museum's closing time.
Do I need to book in advance — and why avoid resellers?
Yes — all visitors must book a timed entry slot, including those entitled to free admission. Book exclusively at billetterie.musee-orangerie.fr. The museum officially warns against "skip-the-line" ticket sites, mirror websites, and street vendors — reseller tickets risk being refused at the door. There is no legitimate reason to pay a markup: the official site is fast, available in English, and cheaper.
