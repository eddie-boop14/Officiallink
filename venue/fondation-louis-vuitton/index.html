About

Opened in October 2014, the Fondation Louis Vuitton is one of Paris's most extraordinary buildings — twelve billowing glass sails wrapped around a timber-and-steel hull, designed by Frank Gehry and commissioned by LVMH chairman Bernard Arnault. Built on the site of a former bowling alley in the Bois de Boulogne, it sits partly over water, with reflecting pools and a Grotto level that reveal the full scale of the structure from below.

The foundation hosts major temporary exhibitions from the LVMH corporate collection and international loans — past shows have covered Basquiat, Warhol, Monet, Rothko, and Gerhard Richter. From April 15 to August 16, 2026, the featured exhibition is Calder. Rêver en équilibre, a centenary retrospective of Alexander Calder's work. The rooftop terraces offer views across the Bois and over the Paris skyline toward La Défense. A free electric shuttle connects the entrance to Place Charles de Gaulle every 20 minutes.

FAQ
How much does Fondation Louis Vuitton cost?
The standard adult ticket is €18, booked direct at fondationlouisvuitton.fr. Under-26s and students pay €10. Under-18s pay €5. A family ticket (2 adults + up to 4 under-18s) costs €36. Under-3s enter free. Students with a valid international student card also get free entry every Thursday. Booking online is strongly recommended as timed slots sell out, especially on weekends.
What are the opening hours and which day is it closed?
The Fondation is open Monday and Wednesday through Sunday, typically 12pm to 7pm (often extended to 8pm on weekends during major exhibitions). It is closed every Tuesday. Hours can vary by season and exhibition — always confirm at fondationlouisvuitton.fr before you go. Last entry is 15 minutes before closing.
How do I get there — is the shuttle really free?
Yes, the electric shuttle is free and included with your admission ticket. It departs every 20 minutes from Exit 2 of Charles de Gaulle–Étoile metro station (Line 1), at 44 Avenue de Friedland. The nearest metro stop on foot is Les Sablons (Line 1), about 950m away. There is no car park — public transport or the shuttle is the practical option.
