About

Sverd i Fjell (Swords in Rock) is a public monument at Møllebukta on the shores of Hafrsfjord, in the Madla borough of Stavanger, approximately 6 km southwest of the city centre. The monument consists of three large bronze swords embedded point-downward into the natural bedrock of a small hill beside the fjord. The tallest sword stands 10 metres; construction ran from 1980 to 1983. The swords were cast from bronze with hilts modelled on authentic Viking-era sword examples found in various parts of Norway. The monument was unveiled by King Olav V of Norway in 1983.

The monument was created by sculptor Fritz Røed (1928–2002) from Bryne to commemorate the Battle of Hafrsfjord in 872 AD, when King Harald Fairhair (Harald Hårfagre) defeated the assembled petty kings of western Norway and unified the country under one crown. The largest sword represents the victorious Harald; the two shorter swords represent the defeated rulers. The battle is documented in Snorre Sturlason's Heimskringla, the best-known collection of Old Norse kings' sagas. The decision to embed the swords permanently into solid bedrock carries symbolic weight: once planted, they cannot be removed — representing peace that cannot be broken.

The site at Møllebukta is a popular recreational area year-round. A walking and cycling path runs along the fjord from the monument. Møllebukta bay is a summer bathing area with grass, beach, and a freshwater stream from lake Store Stokkavatn. A seasonal kiosk operates near the parking area. Public toilets are available near the main car park. The site is exposed to coastal weather; wind and rain are possible at any time of year. Early morning and late afternoon provide the clearest light for the monument against the fjord. The entire Hafrsfjord area contains prehistoric finds including Bronze Age petroglyphs at Fluberget in Hestnes.

FAQ
Is Swords in Rock free to visit?
Yes. Sverd i Fjell is a free public monument with no entry fee, ticket, or booking required. The site is open year-round at all hours. Free parking is available at Møllebukta adjacent to the monument. Any charge offered online is for a guided tour, not for access to the monument itself.
How do I get there from Stavanger city centre?
By car: approximately 15 minutes southwest via Fv509 (Revheimsveien) — free parking at Møllebukta. By bus: Kolumbus lines 2, 3, 6, and 29 serve stops within a short walk — check kolumbus.no for current timetables. By foot or bicycle: approximately 6 km along the fjord path from the city centre.
