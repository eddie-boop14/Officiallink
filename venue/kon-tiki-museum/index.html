About

The Kon-Tiki Museum opened in Oslo in 1949, following Thor Heyerdahl's (1914–2002) Pacific crossing on the balsa wood raft Kon-Tiki. In 1947, Heyerdahl and a five-man crew sailed 8,000 km from Peru to the Polynesian islands over 101 days. The original raft remains the museum's centerpiece. Since 1950 close to 20 million people have visited the museum. The Oscar-winning Kon-Tiki documentary is screened daily at noon.

Also on display is the Ra II — a papyrus reed boat that Heyerdahl sailed across the Atlantic from Morocco to Barbados in 1970, becoming the first vessel to do so under a United Nations flag. The Tigris reed boat is also exhibited. Further displays include Easter Island artifacts, Heyerdahl's personal archive and library of approximately 8,000 books, an underground cave exhibit (30 metres), and an underwater display featuring a 10-metre whale shark model.

Norwegian school classes and kindergarten groups enter free when accompanied by a teacher or adult. Guided tours in Norwegian and English are available for groups of up to 30, lasting approximately 50 minutes. The museum is at Bygdøynesveien 36, directly adjacent to the Fram Museum and within walking distance of the Norwegian Maritime Museum. Combined tickets are available on-site at any of the three museums.

FAQ
How much do Kon-Tiki Museum tickets cost?
Adults €15.50 (180 NOK), Children (6–17) €6.00 (70 NOK), Seniors (67+) €12.00 (140 NOK), Students €6.00 (70 NOK, valid ID required), Family (2 adults + 5 children) €34.00 (400 NOK), Groups of 10+ €11.00 per person (130 NOK). Free with Oslo Pass. Norwegian school classes and kindergartens enter free with a teacher. Purchase at the entrance or online at booking.duell.no.
What are the Kon-Tiki Museum opening hours?
September–April: 10:00–17:00 daily. May: 10:00–18:00 daily. June–August: 09:30–18:00 daily. Open on Christmas Eve and New Year's Eve until 15:00. Open all year round, no regular closures.
What is on display at Kon-Tiki Museum?
The original Kon-Tiki balsa raft (1947 Pacific crossing, Peru to Polynesia), the Ra II papyrus boat (1970 Atlantic crossing), the Tigris reed boat, Easter Island artifacts, Heyerdahl's personal library (~8,000 books), an underground cave exhibit (30 m), an underwater exhibit with a 10-metre whale shark model, and a Tiki pop culture display. The Oscar-winning Kon-Tiki documentary is screened daily at noon.
How do I get to Kon-Tiki Museum from central Oslo?
Bus 30 runs every 10 minutes from Jernbanetorget, City Hall, and Solli Plass to the Bygdøynes stop. Check schedules at ruter.no. April–October: ferry from Rådhuskaien 4 every 20 minutes to Bygdøynes (stop 2), approximately 200 metres from the museum. By car: public parking at Bygdøynesveien 37.
Are combined tickets available with other Bygdøy museums?
Yes, on-site at any of the three museums — not available online. Kon-Tiki + Fram Museum: Adults €27.50 (325 NOK), Seniors €21.50 (250 NOK), Children/Students €10.50 (125 NOK), Family €61.50 (720 NOK). Kon-Tiki + Norwegian Maritime Museum: Adults €27.50 (325 NOK), Seniors €21.50 (250 NOK). All three museums: Adults €41.50 (485 NOK), Seniors €32.50 (380 NOK).
