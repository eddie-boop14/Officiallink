About

Polaria was established in 1998 as a non-profit foundation at Hjalmar Johansens gate 12, Tromsø. Its building, designed by JAF Arkitektkontor AS, consists of angular overlapping panels intended to evoke ice floes pushed ashore by the Arctic Ocean — making it one of Tromsø's most recognisable structures. Polaria holds the distinction of being the world's northernmost aquarium. All exhibitions are developed in collaboration with researchers at the Fram Centre (Fram — High North Research Centre for Climate and the Environment).

The aquarium houses seven seals: three bearded seals, three harbour seals, and one ringed seal. Polaria is one of only two places in Europe where visitors can see bearded seals up close. Feeding and training sessions take place daily at 10:30, 12:30, and 15:30 — these are regular enrichment activities, not staged shows. The aquarium section also holds cod, halibut, wolffish, king crab, and jellyfish. The Panoramic Theatre screens three films covering the Northern Lights, Svalbard's glaciers, and aquarium operations. A major expansion completed in 2025 tripled the seal pool area and added panoramic windows.

Interactive exhibitions focus on Arctic climate, marine ecosystems, plastic pollution, and the Svalbard food web. The mission of the Polaria Foundation is to communicate research-based knowledge about the Arctic and promote sustainable management of the region. Polaria is Eco-Lighthouse certified (Miljøfyrtårn). A café and gift shop are on-site. Tromsø residents can receive free admission by presenting proof of residence — the Ambassador Ticket scheme allows one Tromsø resident per group to enter free when accompanied by out-of-town guests.

Best time: Monday and Tuesday mornings before 12:00 are the least crowded. Arrive before the 10:30 seal feeding to secure a good position near the pool without the midday rush. Weekends draw families and are consistently the busiest periods. The 15:30 feeding attracts fewer visitors than the 10:30 and 12:30 sessions — this is the quietest feeding slot on weekdays.
FAQ
How much does Polaria cost?
Adults NOK 395 (approx. €34.00). Children aged 3–15 pay NOK 220 (approx. €19.00). Children under 3 are free. Students with valid ID pay NOK 260 (approx. €22.50). Seniors 67+ pay NOK 260 (approx. €22.50). A family ticket (2 adults + 3 children) costs NOK 925 (approx. €79.50). Book direct at billett.polaria.no — reseller sites charge significantly more for the same admission.
What are Polaria's opening hours?
Polaria is open daily 10:00–17:00 year-round. Seal feeding sessions take place every day at 10:30, 12:30, and 15:30. These sessions are part of the animals' regular enrichment programme and are open for visitors to watch at no extra charge.
What animals can I see at Polaria?
Polaria houses seven seals: three bearded seals, three harbour seals, and one ringed seal. It is one of two places in Europe where visitors can see bearded seals up close. The aquarium section holds Arctic marine species including cod, halibut, wolffish, king crab, and jellyfish. The seal pool was expanded significantly in 2025 with panoramic windows and outdoor spaces added.
What is included in the admission ticket?
One admission ticket gives access to the entire venue: all interactive exhibitions, the Panoramic Theatre (three Arctic films), the seal pool, and the aquarium tanks. There is no additional charge for the film screenings or the seal feeding sessions. A café and gift shop are also on-site.
Can I book Polaria tickets online?
Yes. Book directly at billett.polaria.no for confirmed admission. Coop Nord, Ishavskraft, and iTromsø customers are eligible for a 50% discount on adult and child tickets when purchasing online — confirm at arrival.
