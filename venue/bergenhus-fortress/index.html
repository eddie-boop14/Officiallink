About

Bergenhus Fortress (Bergenhus Festning) occupies the entrance to Bergen harbour, adjacent to the UNESCO-listed Bryggen wharf. It is one of Norway's oldest and best-preserved stone fortifications, with excavations revealing building foundations believed to predate 1100. The fortress contains two principal historic buildings managed by Bymuseet i Bergen: Håkon's Hall and the Rosenkrantz Tower. The wider fortress grounds — including the Koengen concert area and rampart walks — are freely accessible 07:00–23:00 daily. The grounds house approximately 220 military and civilian personnel and remain an active military installation under the Royal Norwegian Navy.

Håkon's Hall was built between 1247 and 1261 by King Håkon Håkonsson as a royal residence and banquet hall, and is the largest secular medieval building still standing in Norway at 37 x 16 metres. When Håkon's son Magnus married Danish princess Ingeborg in 1261, 2,000 guests were accommodated across three buildings on the site. Bergen was then Norway's largest city and political capital. The hall was severely damaged on 20 April 1944 when a Dutch ammunition ship, the Voorbode, exploded in the harbour directly below; only the walls remained. Restoration was completed in the 1950s. The hall continues to host royal dinners and official state occasions.

The Rosenkrantz Tower incorporates masonry from the 1270s and was substantially rebuilt in the 1560s by Erik Rosenkrantz, governor of Bergen Castle, as a combined residence and fortified tower. It is regarded as the most important Renaissance monument in Norway. Visitors climb seven floors through a dungeon, guards' room, the chapel with its original altar, King Magnus Lagabøte's bedchamber, and reach the rooftop cannon attic with harbour views. Recommended visit time is 45–50 minutes. Children under 14 must be accompanied by an adult. Tickets for both museums are available at shop.bymuseet.no or on site.

FAQ
Is Bergenhus Fortress free to visit?
The fortress grounds are free to enter and open 07:00–23:00 daily. Tickets are required to enter the interior of Håkon's Hall (NOK 120 / ~€10.50 adult, children 0–17 free) and the Rosenkrantz Tower (NOK 170 / ~€15.00 adult). Buy tickets at bymuseet.no or at the museums on site.
What are the opening hours for Håkon's Hall?
Håkon's Hall is open 2 January – 20 December. It is closed on various dates due to official events — see bymuseet.no for the full list of closure dates. The Rosenkrantz Tower remains open on most dates when Håkon's Hall is closed, except public holidays.
How do I get to Bergenhus Fortress?
Bergenhus Fortress is located at the end of Bryggen wharf, approximately 3 minutes walk from Bryggen. The nearest bus stops are Bradbenken, Sandbrogaten, and Bontelabo. A taxi stand is located just outside the Main Gate at Bradbenken.
What is Håkon's Hall?
Håkon's Hall is the largest secular medieval building still standing in Norway, measuring 37 x 16 metres. It was built between 1247 and 1261 by King Håkon Håkonsson as a royal residence and banquet hall. The hall was severely damaged in 1944 when a Dutch ammunition ship exploded in Bergen harbour, and was restored to its current state in the 1950s.
What is the Rosenkrantz Tower?
The Rosenkrantz Tower is regarded as the most important Renaissance monument in Norway. Parts of the structure date to the 1270s. It was substantially rebuilt in the 1560s by Erik Rosenkrantz, governor of Bergen Castle, as a combined residence and fortified tower. Visitors can climb through seven floors including the dungeon, royal chambers, a chapel, and reach the roof with city views. Allow 45–50 minutes. Adult NOK 170 (~€15.00), children under 14 must be accompanied by an adult.
