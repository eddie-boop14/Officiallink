About

Paella valenciana originated in the rice-farming communities surrounding the Albufera lagoon, south of Valencia. The dish was originally cooked by agricultural labourers using whatever was available — short-grain rice grown in the surrounding paddies, chicken, rabbit, fresh green beans (ferradura), and butter beans (garrofó). Saffron, sweet paprika, rosemary, olive oil, and water complete the traditional ingredient list. The pan is cooked over an open wood fire, which produces the prized layer of caramelised rice at the bottom called socarrat.

The village of El Palmar, inside Albufera Natural Park 15 km south of Valencia, is the recognised centre of paella culture. The village contains over a dozen arrocerías (rice restaurants) run by families with multi-generational paella traditions. Notable establishments include Restaurante Bon Aire (Calle Capdet, 41 — winner of the Concurso Internacional de Paella Valenciana), Arroceria Maribel (Calle Francisco Monleón, 5 — Michelin Bib Gourmand, also serves vegetarian paella), El Graner (Calle Vicent Baldoví, 14), and Pasqualet. Prices at these restaurants run €18–28 per person; minimum order is typically two persons per pan.

Paella is served at lunch only — kitchens generally begin service at 13:00 and stop taking orders by 15:30–16:00. Weekend bookings at popular restaurants require 2–4 weeks advance notice. All El Palmar restaurants can be booked directly by phone, in Spanish. No intermediary booking platform is needed. Bus 25 from Valencia city centre runs to El Palmar in approximately 45 minutes; only one bus per hour returns, so check the return schedule before eating.

Restaurants include Bon Aire (Calle Capdet 41), Arroceria Maribel (Michelin Bib Gourmand, Calle Francisco Monleón 5), El Graner (Calle Vicent Baldoví 14), and Pasqualet. What ingredients are in a true paella valenciana?
Authentic paella valenciana contains short-grain rice, chicken, rabbit, flat green beans (ferradura), butter beans (garrofó), tomato, olive oil, saffron, sweet paprika, rosemary, and water. Snails and duck are optional traditional additions. Mixed seafood or chorizo paella is not classified as authentic Valencian paella by the Valencian regional authorities.
