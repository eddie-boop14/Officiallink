About

The Oslo Opera House (Operahuset) opened on 12 April 2008 and is the home of the Norwegian National Opera and Ballet (Den Norske Opera & Ballett). Designed by the architecture firm Snøhetta, the building's angular white marble and granite roof slopes down into the Oslofjord to resemble a glacier. It is Norway's largest cultural building constructed since the Middle Ages, containing three stages and over 1,000 rooms. The building is located in the Bjørvika waterfront district at Kirsten Flagstads plass 1.

The roof is a publicly accessible plaza at all times. Inside, the building features eight commissioned art installations, including perforated wall panels by Olafur Eliasson and a main stage curtain designed to resemble crumpled foil. The four-storey oak Wave Wall wraps around the three performance spaces. The box office and indoor public areas are open Monday–Saturday 11:00–16:00 and Sunday 12:00–16:00, or until the end of any evening performance.

Guided tours last approximately 50 minutes and cover the main stage, costume workshop, scene painting room, and backstage areas. Tours are in English on weekends, with additional slots in June, July, and August (2–3 per day). Tickets release every Tuesday for that same week via operaen.no. Maximum 25 persons per group. Private group tours are no longer offered. Two restaurants are on site: Havsmak and Brasserie Opera. Last updated: March 2026 · Verified

FAQ
Is the Oslo Opera House free to visit?
Yes. The building exterior and walkable marble roof are free public spaces accessible at all times. Indoor public areas are open during box office hours at no charge. Guided backstage tours cost NOK 180 per adult (approx. €16.00) and NOK 105 per child, available on weekends via operaen.no. Any site selling "entry tickets" to walk the roof is selling access to a free public area.
When are English guided tours available at the Oslo Opera House?
English guided tours run on Saturdays and Sundays. In June, July, and August there are 2–3 English slots per day. Tickets go on sale every Tuesday for that same week and regularly sell out. Maximum 25 persons per tour. Book at operaen.no — private group tours are no longer offered.
What are the Oslo Opera House opening hours?
Box office and indoor public areas: Monday–Saturday 11:00–16:00, Sunday 12:00–16:00, or until the end of any evening performance. The outdoor roof and plaza are accessible as a public space at all times.
Where is the Oslo Opera House located?
Kirsten Flagstads plass 1, Bjørvika waterfront district, Oslo. Approximately 5–10 minutes walk from Oslo Central Station (Oslo S). Trams and buses serve the Tollboden and Bjørvika area. Bicycle parking is available along Operagaten. Disabled parking is in Operagaten (north side), 150 metres from the main entrance.
What does a guided tour of the Oslo Opera House include?
Tours last approximately 50 minutes and include the main stage, costume workshop, scene painting room, and backstage areas. The guide covers architecture, the eight art installations, stage technology, and opera and ballet production. Maximum 25 persons per group. Private group tours are no longer available.
