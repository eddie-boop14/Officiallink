About

The Norwegian Petroleum Museum (Norsk Oljemuseum) opened on 20 May 1999 at Kjeringholmen 1A in central Stavanger, at the harbour (Vågen). The building was designed by Lunde & Løvseth Arkitekter following a national competition; its form references Norwegian coastal bedrock and offshore installations. Covering approximately 5,000 square metres, it houses exhibition space, a cinema, restaurant, specialist library, meeting rooms, workshops, and offices. From the water, the building resembles a small oil platform and has become a recognisable element of the Stavanger waterfront.

The permanent exhibitions document how the discovery of oil in the North Sea in 1969 altered Norway's economy and society. Content covers the geology of oil and gas formation, exploration methods, drilling technology, daily life on offshore platforms, and the environmental implications of petroleum production. Exhibits include full-scale models of drilling equipment and platform modules, original artefacts, films, photographs, and interactive stations. The Troll A platform — at 472 metres the world's tallest movable human-built structure — features prominently. In December 2023 the museum was certified as an Eco-Lighthouse.

Children's activities include a rescue chute, catastrophe simulation room, and a play platform for younger visitors. A free paper guide is available at the entrance in 11 languages. Guided tours are available at additional cost, with group sizes limited to 20 per guide — these must be pre-arranged via norskolje.museum.no. The museum's specialist library holds a large collection on oil, gas, technology, and industry. Nearest parking is Jorenholmen garage along the quay.

FAQ
How much does the Norwegian Petroleum Museum cost?
Adult: €15.50 (NOK 180). Children 4–16: €5.50 (NOK 60). Children under 4: free. Family (2 adults + 3 children): €36.50 (NOK 420). Students: €8.00 (NOK 90). Pensioners 65+: €12.00 (NOK 140). Buy tickets at norskolje.museum.no/en/price.
What are the opening hours?
1 Jun–31 Aug: daily 10:00–19:00. 1 Sep–31 May: Mon–Sat 10:00–16:00, Sun 10:00–18:00. Closed 24, 25, 31 Dec and 1 Jan. Closed Good Friday and Easter Sunday. Closed 17 May. Confirm at norskolje.museum.no/en/home/visit-the-museum/opening-hours.
Where is the museum?
Kjeringholmen 1A, Stavanger city centre, at the harbour (Vågen). The building is visible from the water and resembles a small offshore platform. Nearest parking is Jorenholmen garage along the Stavanger quay.
What are the main exhibits?
Full-scale drilling equipment models, platform module recreations, the Troll A platform documentation (472 m, world's tallest movable structure), a cinema, interactive geology and drilling exhibits, and children's activities including a rescue chute and catastrophe room. A free paper guide is available in 11 languages.
Are guided tours available?
Yes, at additional cost and must be booked in advance. Group sizes are limited to 20 participants per guide. Contact the museum via norskolje.museum.no to arrange. A free self-guided paper brochure in 11 languages is available at the entrance without pre-booking.
