About

The Royal Palace (Det Kongelige Slott) was completed in 1849 as a residence for King Carl Johan, though he did not live to see its completion. King Oscar I was the first monarch to reside there. The Neoclassical building sits at the top of Karl Johans gate, Oslo's main boulevard, at Slottsplassen 1. It remains the official residence and primary workplace of the Norwegian monarch, and houses the offices of the Royal Family and most of the Royal Court.

Each summer, the Palace opens a selection of state rooms for guided tours. The 2026 tour begins in the Ministers Salon and continues through the Council Chamber, White Drawing Room, Bernadotte Salon, Upper Vestibule, Smoking Room, and Grand Ballroom. The Banqueting Hall accommodates over 200 guests during state dinners. Tours last approximately 45 minutes, departing every 20 minutes. Five English-language tours run daily at 12:00, 12:20, 14:00, 14:20, and 16:00. All visitors must follow a guide; private guides and personal translators are not permitted.

The ticket includes same-day entry to Queen Sonja Art Stable at Parkveien 50. Cameras, handbags, coats, and umbrellas must be left in cloakroom lockers. Photography is not permitted inside the Palace. The Palace Park (Slottsparken) is open year-round at no charge. The Changing of the Guard takes place daily at 13:30 and is free to observe. FAQ
How much does a guided tour of the Royal Palace Oslo cost?
Adult NOK 230 (approx. €20.00). Student and children aged 6–17 NOK 110 (approx. €10.00). Children under 6 free. Wheelchair ticket NOK 110; accompanying carers free. Closed groups up to 40 persons NOK 6,500 total. Tickets via ticketmaster.no as directed by royalcourt.no. A small number of tickets are also available at the entrance daily.
When is the Royal Palace open to visitors in 2026?
The 2026 season runs 20 June to 16 August, open daily 10:00–17:00 (last admission). Tours depart every 20 minutes. Outside this period the Palace is closed to the public. The Palace Park surrounding the building is open year-round at no charge.
Are English-language tours available at the Royal Palace Oslo?
Yes. Five English tours run daily: 12:00, 12:20, 14:00, 14:20, and 16:00. All other tours are in Norwegian and depart every 20 minutes from 10:00. Private guides and personal translators are not permitted on tours.
Is photography allowed inside the Royal Palace Oslo?
Photography is not permitted inside the Royal Palace. Cameras must be left in the cloakroom lockers along with handbags, coats, and umbrellas. The Palace Park and exterior are unrestricted. Mobile phones must be on silent mode during the tour.
What is included in the Royal Palace ticket?
The ticket covers the guided tour through state rooms (approximately 45 minutes) and same-day entry to Queen Sonja Art Stable at Parkveien 50. An outdoor café is in the Stable Yard. A gift shop is at the end of the tour in the Vestibule. Wheelchair-accessible toilets are available inside the Palace.
