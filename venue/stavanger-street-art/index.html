About

The Nuart Festival has been held annually in Stavanger since 2001, inviting internationally recognised artists to create large-scale murals, interventions, and installations across the city. Nuart is widely regarded as one of the world's leading street art festivals and is an official Google Arts & Culture partner. Works span the full spectrum of the genre: situationism, graffiti, post-graffiti, muralism, comic culture, stencil art, and activist imagery. Artists who have worked in Stavanger include Ernest Zacharevic, Martin Whatson, Dolk, Dan Witz, David Choe, Eine, Ella & Pitr, Evol, Isaac Cordal, Logan Hicks, and many others. All permanent works are free to view at any time of day, every day of the year.

Murals are concentrated across the city centre and the historic Gamle Stavanger district, with additional pieces in residential and industrial neighbourhoods. The official Nuart map (available at nuartfestival.no and from the Stavanger tourist information office) documents the locations of major works. Nuart's Google Arts & Culture page allows visitors to research works and artists before arriving. Each year during the first week of September the festival adds new pieces to the city's collection, meaning the trail changes and grows annually. The festival has placed Stavanger on the international street art map alongside cities including Bristol, Berlin, and Melbourne.

Guided Nuart Street Art Walking Tours run through Stavanger covering over 20 years of festival history and last approximately 1.5 hours. These are operated by Nuart via streetarttours.no and can be contacted at info@streetarttours.no. Self-guided exploration using the official map requires no booking and costs nothing. Comfortable footwear is recommended; the full trail covers substantial ground across the city. Stavanger's weather is variable — waterproof clothing is advisable at any time of year.

Best time: Early morning on weekdays — murals are accessible before other foot traffic builds. The art is outdoors 24 hours so early morning or late evening on long summer days gives good light and fewer people. The first week of September (Nuart Festival) brings higher visitor numbers to specific works in progress. Winter weekdays are the quietest period overall.
FAQ
Is Stavanger Street Art free?
Yes. All permanent murals across Stavanger are free to view at any time of day, year-round. No ticket, booking, or entry fee is required. The Nuart Festival may host some ticketed indoor events during September — check nuartfestival.no for any current programmes.
Where do I find the official street art map?
The official Nuart map is available at nuartfestival.no and from the Stavanger tourist information office. Nuart is also an official Google Arts & Culture partner — works can be viewed and researched via the Google Arts & Culture app before visiting.
When is the Nuart Festival?
The Nuart Festival has historically taken place in the first week of September, when invited artists create new works across the city. All permanent murals from previous years remain visible year-round. Check nuartfestival.no for the current year's programme dates.
Are guided tours available?
Yes. Nuart Street Art Walking Tours cover over 20 years of festival history and last approximately 1.5 hours. Book via streetarttours.no or email info@streetarttours.no. Self-guided tours using the official Nuart map are free and require no booking.
Which artists are represented?
Represented artists include Ernest Zacharevic, Martin Whatson, Dolk, Dan Witz, David Choe, Eine, Ella & Pitr, Evol, Isaac Cordal, Logan Hicks, Pure Evil, Swoon, and Sandra Chevrier, among many others. The full and current list with locations is at nuartfestival.no.
