About

Torres de Serranos are two polygonal Gothic towers built in 1392–1398 by architect Pere Balaguer on the orders of King Peter IV of Aragon. They formed the northern gate of Valencia's medieval city wall, serving as the main entrance for travellers arriving from Aragón. The towers were declared a National Monument in 1871 and survived the demolition of the surrounding medieval walls in 1865. They are among the largest surviving Gothic city gates in Europe.

The towers served multiple functions across the centuries. From the 16th to the 19th century they were used as a prison for nobles and knights. During the Spanish Civil War (1936–1939), the Torres de Serranos were used to safeguard artworks evacuated from Madrid's Prado Museum. The Gothic architecture features pointed arches and ribbed vaults visible from the rear facade, which opens outward unlike the solid defensive front. The terraces provide views over the Turia Garden and the historic centre.

Torres de Serranos is the annual setting for the Crida, the traditional proclamation that opens the Las Fallas festival each year on the last Sunday of February. The Fallera Mayor (Fallas Queen) delivers the address from the towers. The site is located at Plaza de los Fueros, s/n, 46003 Valencia, on the edge of the historic El Carmen neighbourhood. Visits may be restricted during adverse weather conditions including rain, fog, or strong wind.

FAQ
How much does Torres de Serranos cost?
General admission is €2. Reduced tickets (groups, students with Jove card, pensioners, children aged 7–12) are €1. Entry is free every Sunday and on public holidays. Tickets are sold at valencia.es — reseller platforms charge significant markup for the same ticket.
What are the opening hours?
Monday to Saturday 10:00–19:00 (last entry 18:30). Sundays and public holidays 10:00–14:00 (last entry 13:30). Closed 1 January, 6 January, 1 May, and 25 December. Visits may also be restricted during adverse weather (rain, fog, strong wind).
Is Torres de Serranos free on Sundays?
Yes. Entry is free every Sunday and on all public holidays. Note that Sunday hours are shorter: 10:00–14:00 only. The site is typically busiest on free days, so arrive early to avoid queues.
How do I get to Torres de Serranos?
Take bus lines 6, 16, 26, 28, 94, 95, or C2 to the stop at Plaza de los Fueros. The towers are at Plaza de los Fueros s/n, 46003 Valencia, on the northern edge of the El Carmen neighbourhood. From Valencia's main train station (Estación del Norte) it is approximately a 15-minute walk.
Do I need to book tickets in advance?
Advance booking is available at valencia.es. Walk-up entry is available during opening hours, subject to capacity. Holders of the Valencia Tourist Card enter free — the card is sold at visitvalencia.com. Do not buy tickets through reseller platforms such as GetYourGuide or Viator — they charge €10–€25+ for a €2 ticket.
