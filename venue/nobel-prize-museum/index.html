About

The Nobel Peace Center opened in 2005 and is the museum dedicated to the Nobel Peace Prize. It occupies a former railway station building at Brynjulf Bulls plass 1 on the City Hall Square, Aker Brygge waterfront, Oslo. The museum is an independent not-for-profit foundation financed through private donations and government grants. Each year it welcomes approximately 200,000 visitors and over 700 school groups.

The permanent Nobel Field installation displays all Peace Prize laureates since 1901. Changing exhibitions — two to three per year — cover laureates' work and current peace-related topics. An audio guide is available in Norwegian, English, Spanish, French, and German via visitors' own smartphones. Weekend guided tours in English and Norwegian are included in the entrance ticket. Tours run Saturday and Sunday at 12:00, 13:00, 14:00, and 15:00; each lasts approximately 30 minutes with a maximum of 30 people. During Easter (28 March–6 April 2026), daily guided tours are available.

The museum is accessible for physically impaired visitors, with a ramp at the entrance and an elevator between the two floors. Self-service lockers are available. Food and drinks may not be consumed in exhibition areas. The museum does not have a café. On Wednesdays, the exhibitions are open until 20:00 and adult tickets are half price from 17:00. The museum is closed on Mondays except during Norwegian school holidays. Last updated: March 2026 · Verified

FAQ
How much does the Nobel Peace Center cost?
Adult NOK 180 (approx. €16.00). Student NOK 100 (approx. €9.00). Children aged 0–17 free. On Wednesdays from 17:00, adult tickets are half price (NOK 90). Free with a valid Oslo Pass. Book at ticket.nobelpeacecenter.org or pay at the door.
What are the Nobel Peace Center opening hours?
Tuesday–Sunday 11:00–17:00. Wednesday 11:00–20:00. Closed Mondays, except during Norwegian school holidays. Closed 24, 25, and 31 December. Altered hours around 9–11 December each year for Nobel Prize ceremonies. Easter 2026: open daily 28 March–6 April.
Are guided tours included in the Nobel Peace Center ticket?
Weekend guided tours are included in the entrance ticket. Register at the museum on arrival. Saturday and Sunday at 12:00, 13:00, 14:00, and 15:00. Each tour lasts approximately 30 minutes. Maximum 30 people per tour. During Easter, daily guided tours are available.
Is the Nobel Peace Center closed during Nobel Prize week?
The museum operates on altered hours around 9–11 December each year when the Nobel Peace Prize ceremony takes place in Oslo. Check nobelpeacecenter.org for exact dates and hours for the current year before planning a December visit.
Where is the Nobel Peace Center located?
Brynjulf Bulls plass 1, 0250 Oslo — City Hall Square, Aker Brygge waterfront. 100 metres from Aker Brygge tram stop (line 12). 100 metres from Aker Brygge ferry terminal. 200 metres from Dokkveien bus stop (lines 32, 33, 54, 81, 169). 700 metres from Nationaltheatret station. Use city centre car parks — no dedicated museum parking.
