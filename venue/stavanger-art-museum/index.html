About

Stavanger Art Museum (Stavanger Kunstmuseum) is part of Museum Stavanger (MUST) and holds a collection of over 3,500 works of Norwegian fine art spanning from the early 19th century to the present day. The museum is situated in Mosvannsparken on the west side of the lake Mosvatnet, approximately 3 km from Stavanger city centre at Henrik Ibsensgt. 55. The building was developed from the earlier institution Stavanger Faste Galleri, which received its first fixed premises in 1893. The museum was renamed Stavanger Kunstmuseum in 2010.

The permanent collection places particular emphasis on Lars Hertervig (Norway's largest single-institution Hertervig collection), Kitty Kielland, and Frida Hansen. Additional significant holdings include the Jan Groth and Steingrim Laursen collection established in 1998, and 67 contemporary works gifted by Trond Mohn in 2012. The museum produces 3 to 5 temporary exhibitions annually, featuring Norwegian and international art across historical and contemporary genres. Annual visitor numbers have been approximately 40,000–50,000 in recent years.

Since 2019 the museum has incorporated NONA, a dedicated space with Studio Nona workshop and an exhibition area focused on interaction between artworks and visitors. One ticket grants admission to all MUST museums open on the same day, including the Norwegian Canning Museum, Stavanger Maritime Museum, and others. Children under 18 and students with valid ID enter free. Annual passes covering all MUST museums with one guest are available at NOK 700 (approx. €61.00).

FAQ
How much does Stavanger Art Museum cost?
Adult admission is €14.50 (NOK 170). Children and young people under 18 enter free. Students with valid ID enter free. One ticket covers all MUST museums open on the same day. Buy tickets at billett.museumstavanger.no or at the museum entrance.
What are the opening hours?
1 Jan–31 May: Mon closed, Tue–Wed 11:00–15:00, Thu 11:00–19:00, Fri 11:00–15:00, Sat–Sun 11:00–16:00. 1 Jun–15 Aug: daily 10:00–16:00. Always verify current hours at museumstavanger.no/en/åpningstider before visiting.
Where is Stavanger Art Museum?
The museum is at Henrik Ibsensgt. 55 in Mosvannsparken, about 3 km from Stavanger city centre. On foot from the centre: approximately 35 minutes. By bus: route 8 stops at Hertervigs Plass. Parking is available at P-Jernbanen.
What is the permanent collection?
Over 3,500 works with emphasis on Lars Hertervig (Norway's largest institutional Hertervig collection), Kitty Kielland, and Frida Hansen. Additional holdings include the Jan Groth and Steingrim Laursen collection and 67 contemporary works gifted by Trond Mohn in 2012.
Does one ticket cover multiple MUST museums?
Yes. One adult ticket (€14.50 / NOK 170) grants access to all MUST museums open on the same day — including the Norwegian Canning Museum, Stavanger Maritime Museum, and Stavanger Museum. Buy tickets at billett.museumstavanger.no.
