About

The Hanseatic Museum and Schøtstuene (Det Hanseatiske Museum og Schøtstuene) is part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Bryggen, which was inscribed on the World Heritage List in 1979. The museum occupies preserved wooden buildings in the Bryggen area and documents how Hanseatic merchants from northern Germany established and operated a trading office in Bergen from 1360 to 1761. The Hanseatic merchants controlled the stockfish trade between Bergen and continental Europe — fish sourced from northern Norway was exchanged for grain and other goods in a system that made Bryggen one of the most significant trading hubs in northern Europe. The museum has a universal design and is accessible to visitors with mobility needs.

The Schøtstuene assembly rooms, located at Øvregaten 50 beside St. Mary's Church, are the last surviving Hanseatic assembly rooms in the world. Hanseatic merchants used these rooms for communal meals, meetings and deliberations. The buildings contain paintings of vines dating up to 300 years old and a glass floor section revealing a medieval ruin from approximately 1280, discovered when the museum was founded 148 years ago. A museum gift shop is located at Bredsgården at the end of the guided walking route through Bryggen.

A guided tour of approximately 45 minutes is included in every ticket. Tours begin at Schøtstuene and continue through the narrow passageways of the World Heritage Site. In summer (May–October), guided tours run daily. From January to April, English tours run on Saturdays and Sundays at 13:00, with additional tours on Mondays and Wednesdays at 12:00 and 14:00. Museum opening hours for the summer season (May–October) are daily 10:00–18:00. Winter hours (February–April) are daily 10:00–16:00. Tickets can be purchased at museumsbillett.no or at the museum entrance.

FAQ
How much does the Hanseatic Museum cost?
Adult NOK 160 (~€14.00). Students NOK 80 (~€7.00). Children under 18 are free. Bergen Card holders pay NOK 130 (~€11.50). Group tickets (10+ people) are NOK 130 per person. A guided tour is included in the ticket price. Buy tickets at museumsbillett.no or at the museum entrance at Øvregaten 50.
When is the Hanseatic Museum open?
February–April 2026: daily 10:00–16:00. May–October 2026: Monday–Sunday 10:00–18:00. Closed 17 May. Guided tours in English run daily in summer; on Saturdays, Sundays, Mondays and Wednesdays in the off-season. Check hanseatiskemuseum.museumvest.no for current hours.
Where is the Hanseatic Museum located?
The museum entrance for Schøtstuene is at Øvregaten 50, 5003 Bergen, situated beside St. Mary's Church in the Bryggen World Heritage area. The museum is walkable from Bergen city centre in under 10 minutes.
Is a guided tour included in the ticket?
Yes. A guided tour of approximately 45 minutes is included in the ticket price. Tours start at Schøtstuene and continue through the narrow passageways of Bryggen, ending outside Bredsgården. In summer, guided tours run daily. In the off-season, English tours run on Saturdays and Sundays at 13:00, with additional tours on Mondays and Wednesdays at 12:00 and 14:00.
What is the Schøtstuene?
Schøtstuene are the last surviving Hanseatic assembly rooms in the world. Located beside St. Mary's Church, these rooms were where Hanseatic merchants gathered for meals, meetings and discussions during Bergen's role as a major European trading hub between 1360 and 1761. The buildings include up to 300-year-old vine paintings and a glass floor revealing a medieval ruin from around 1280.
