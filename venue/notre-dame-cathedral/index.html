About

Notre-Dame de Paris is one of the finest examples of French Gothic architecture in the world. Construction began in 1163 under Bishop Maurice de Sully and the cathedral was largely complete by 1345, though it was embellished and modified across the following centuries. Its west façade, three rose windows, flying buttresses, and 96-metre spire made it an architectural landmark long before Victor Hugo's 1831 novel brought it global fame.

On 15 April 2019 a fire destroyed the spire and much of the oak roof structure known as "the Forest," shocking the world. Over 2,000 craftspeople — stonemasons, carpenters, glassmakers, bell-founders — spent five years on a meticulous reconstruction. The cathedral officially reopened on 7–8 December 2024, with its stonework cleaned to a brilliant white, rose windows more vivid than before the fire, and a new liturgical interior featuring bronze furnishings and 1,500 solid oak chairs. The rebuilt spire — identical in form to Viollet-le-Duc's 1859 design — rises once again over the Île de la Cité.

Entry to the cathedral is completely free for everyone. An optional free timed reservation at notredamedeparis.fr reduces your wait but is not mandatory — walk-in access is available, though queues can reach 2–3 hours at weekends. The cathedral accommodates up to 2,500 visitors at any one time. The Towers — with 424 steps and sweeping Paris views including close encounters with the gargoyles and chimeras — reopened in September 2025 and cost €16 per adult. The Treasury (€12, paid on-site) holds the Crown of Thorns and centuries of liturgical objects. Allow 45 minutes to 1.5 hours for the cathedral; add one hour if climbing the towers.

FAQ
Is Notre-Dame Cathedral free to visit?
Yes — completely free for everyone. An optional free timed reservation at notredamedeparis.fr reduces your wait but is not required. Walk-in access is always possible, though queues at weekends can be 2–3 hours. No third-party platform is authorised to sell tickets. If anyone charges for "cathedral access" or "skip-the-line entry," it is a scam — the official skip-the-line is the free reservation itself.
What are Notre-Dame's opening hours?
Monday to Wednesday, Friday, Saturday and Sunday: 7:50–19:00. Thursday: 7:50–22:00. Last entry is 30 minutes before closing. Hours may vary during special celebrations, masses, or events — check notredamedeparis.fr before you visit.
Can I visit the towers and how much do they cost?
Yes — the towers reopened on 20 September 2025 with a completely redesigned visitor route featuring a double-helix staircase. Tickets cost €16 per adult. Free for under-18s, EU residents aged 18–25, and Paris Museum Pass holders. There are 424 steps and no lift — not suitable for visitors with reduced mobility. Tickets must be booked online in advance at tours-notre-dame-de-paris.fr; no on-site sales. Allow about one hour for the tower visit.
