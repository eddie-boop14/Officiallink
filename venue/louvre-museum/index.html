About

The Louvre is the world's largest and most visited art museum, housed in the Louvre Palace — originally a 12th-century fortress built by King Philip II that was expanded into a royal residence across five centuries. It opened as a public museum in 1793 with fewer than 550 paintings; today it holds over 380,000 objects, of which 35,000 are on display across 72,735 m² of galleries spanning eight curatorial departments.

The collection ranges from ancient Mesopotamia and Egypt through classical antiquity, Islamic art, medieval Europe, and on to 19th-century French painting and sculpture. The three most iconic works — Leonardo da Vinci's Mona Lisa, the Venus de Milo, and the Winged Victory of Samothrace — each draw enormous crowds. Allow a minimum of three to four hours for highlights; serious visitors should plan a full day. I.M. Pei's glass Pyramid in the Cour Napoléon, inaugurated in 1989, is the main entrance and a landmark in its own right.

Since 14 January 2026 the Louvre operates a two-tier pricing system: €22 for EEA citizens and residents, and €32 for all other visitors. Free admission applies to under-18s, EEA residents under 26, disabled visitors with one carer, and all visitors on the first Friday of each month after 18:00 (except July and August) and on 14 July. Even free-admission visitors must book a timed slot at ticket.louvre.fr. Any exit from the museum is final — plan your visit before you go.

Best time to visit: Wednesday or Friday evening (open until 21:00) — crowds thin out after 18:00 while daytime visitors leave. Avoid Saturday and school-holiday weekdays entirely if possible. Arriving right at 9:00 on a weekday morning is the next best option. The Mona Lisa room is always crowded regardless of time — visit it first or last.
FAQ
How much does the Louvre cost?
Since 14 January 2026 there are two tiers: €22 for EEA citizens and residents, and €32 for everyone else. Free admission applies to under-18s, EEA residents under 26, and all visitors on the first Friday of the month after 18:00 (except July and August) and on 14 July. Even free visitors must book a timed slot at ticket.louvre.fr. The museum does not offer reduced-rate tickets — it is full price or free.
What are the Louvre's opening hours and closed days?
The Louvre is open Monday, Thursday, Saturday and Sunday 9:00–18:00, and Wednesday and Friday 9:00–21:00. It is closed every Tuesday, and on 1 January, 1 May, and 25 December. Last entry is one hour before closing; galleries are cleared 30 minutes before closing. Some rooms may be temporarily closed for restoration — check the room closures schedule on louvre.fr before you visit.
How do I get to the Louvre by metro?
Take Métro Line 1 or Line 7 to Palais Royal – Musée du Louvre station. Exit 1 leads directly into the Carrousel du Louvre underground entrance. Exit 6 (Carrousel du Louvre) opens at 8:30 and provides a covered underground route into the museum — useful in bad weather and often quicker than the Pyramid queue. From CDG Airport take RER B to Châtelet–Les Halles, then switch to Line 1 (one stop).
