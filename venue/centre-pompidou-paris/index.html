About

The Centre Pompidou — known to Parisians as Beaubourg — opened in 1977 as one of the most radical buildings of the 20th century. Designed by Renzo Piano and Richard Rogers, it turned architecture inside out: structural columns, escalators, ventilation ducts and colour-coded service pipes were placed on the exterior, leaving the interior free for flexible cultural use. The colour coding is still famous — red for circulation, yellow for electricity, blue for air conditioning, white for structure.

The building houses the Musée National d'Art Moderne, Europe's largest collection of modern and contemporary art with over 100,000 works spanning from 1905 to the present. Masterpieces by Picasso, Kandinsky, Matisse, Chagall, Brancusi, Bacon, Klein and Warhol sit alongside the world's most significant holdings of Surrealism and post-war abstraction. At its peak the museum attracted over 3.5 million visitors a year to the permanent collection alone — alongside a further million-plus for the BPI public library and other facilities.

On 22 September 2025, the Beaubourg building closed completely for a €262 million renovation project planned to run until 2030. The works cover asbestos removal from all facades, structural repairs, full energy efficiency upgrades, improved accessibility, and a redesigned visitor experience — including a new rooftop terrace. The renovation is led by Moreau Kusunoki in association with Frida Escobedo Studio. During the closure, the Centre's collection and programming continue through the Constellation partner programme, with exhibitions at the Grand Palais and venues across France and internationally. The Centre Pompidou-Metz branch remains open throughout.

FAQ
Is Centre Pompidou Paris open?
No. The Beaubourg building closed to the public on 22 September 2025 for renovation and will not reopen until 2030. You can still experience Pompidou exhibitions through the Constellation partner programme — in Paris, the Grand Palais is a key partner venue. Check centrepompidou.fr for the current schedule and locations.
When will Centre Pompidou reopen?
The renovated Beaubourg building is planned to reopen in 2030. The €262 million project covers asbestos removal, structural repairs, energy upgrades, improved accessibility, and a new rooftop terrace offering panoramic views over Paris. The renovation is led by architects Moreau Kusunoki and Frida Escobedo Studio.
Where can I see Centre Pompidou exhibitions during the closure?
Through the Constellation programme (2025–2030), exhibitions travel to partner venues across France and internationally. In Paris, the Grand Palais hosts major Pompidou collaborations. Centre Pompidou-Metz remains fully open. Check centrepompidou.fr/en for the complete current programme and booking links for each event.
