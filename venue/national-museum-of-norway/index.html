About

The National Museum (Nasjonalmuseet) opened in its current purpose-built building on 11 June 2022, designed by the German firm Kleihues + Schuwerk on the former Vestbanen site adjacent to Oslo City Hall. It is the largest art museum in Northern Europe by floor area. The building consolidates collections previously held across four separate institutions: the National Gallery, the Museum of Decorative Arts and Design, the Museum of Architecture, and the Museum of Contemporary Art.

The collection spans over 400,000 works covering Norwegian art from antiquity to the present, international art, architecture, design, and decorative arts. Key highlights include Edvard Munch's The Scream (1893), works by Johan Christian Dahl, and extensive applied arts and design holdings. The building's central feature is the Light Hall, a luminous marble-and-glass atrium running along the top of the building. Guided tours and audio guides are available in multiple languages. Non-flash photography is permitted in permanent galleries.

The museum is fully accessible for physically impaired visitors, with step-free access, elevators, and accessible restrooms on all floors. A café and museum shop are on site. Brynjulf Bulls plass 3 is the address. Nationaltheatret station is approximately 5–10 minutes walk; tram line 12 and several bus lines serve the waterfront area nearby.
