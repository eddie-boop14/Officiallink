About

MUNCH opened on 22 October 2021 in a 13-storey building at Edvard Munchs plass 1 in the Bjørvika waterfront district of Oslo. The building tilts 6 degrees toward the Oslofjord. It is the largest museum in Norway by exhibition space and holds the world's largest collection of Edvard Munch's work — over 26,000 items including paintings, prints, drawings, watercolours, sculptures, and personal archives. The collection includes several versions of The Scream and Madonna. One version of The Scream is on public display at any given time, with versions rotated during the day.

The museum operates timed entry. Tickets must be pre-booked at munch.no, particularly during peak season (July–August) when walk-in tickets are not available. An audio guide is available in eight languages including Norwegian, English, German, French, Spanish, Japanese, Chinese, and Sign Language, downloadable on visitors' own devices. Backpacks and bags exceeding 40×35×10 cm must be left in cloakroom lockers. Baby strollers are not permitted in the exhibition halls; loan strollers are available at the museum.

The museum contains a café, bar, and restaurant on the upper floors with views over the Oslofjord. Free Wi-Fi (MUNCH-guest) is available throughout. Free admission is offered every Wednesday from 18:00 to 21:00 as drop-in (tickets collected at the museum) — this does not apply in July or August. The museum is open every day of the year except 1 May.

FAQ
How much do Munch Museum tickets cost?
Adults €19.00 (220 NOK), Under 25 €8.50 (100 NOK), Children 0–17 free. Free admission every Wednesday 18:00–21:00 (drop-in, not in July or August). Free with Oslo Pass. Timed entry — pre-book at munch.no to guarantee admission. Walk-in tickets are available outside July–August but cannot be guaranteed at preferred times.
What are the Munch Museum opening hours?
Sunday–Tuesday: 10:00–18:00. Wednesday–Saturday: 10:00–21:00. Open every day of the year except 1 May. Extended hours Wednesday–Saturday allow evening visits.
Does the Munch Museum have The Scream?
Yes. The museum holds multiple versions of The Scream by Edvard Munch. One version is on public display at any given time, with versions rotated. The collection of 26,000+ works also includes multiple versions of Madonna and spans paintings, prints, drawings, watercolours, sculptures, photographs, and personal archives.
How do I get to the Munch Museum?
The museum is at Edvard Munchs plass 1, Bjørvika, Oslo. A 10-minute walk from Oslo Central Station (Oslo S) along the waterfront, passing the Oslo Opera House. Bus 60 stops at Munchmuseet. Tram 13 stops at Bjørvika. There is no dedicated visitor parking — public transport is recommended. The museum is fully accessible.
Is there free admission at the Munch Museum?
Yes. Every Wednesday from 18:00 to 21:00, admission is free. Tickets are collected at the museum on the day (drop-in). This free evening does not apply in July or August. Children 0–17 are always free. Free entry also applies to Oslo Pass holders, ICOM card holders, and companions/carers with valid documentation.
