About

Troldhaugen is the former residence of Norwegian composer Edvard Grieg (1843–1907) and his wife, singer Nina Grieg. Located at Hop, approximately 8 km south of Bergen city centre, the site was Grieg's summer home for the last 22 years of his life. The 1885 Victorian villa remains largely as Grieg left it and is preserved with original furnishings, instruments, and personal effects. Troldhaugen opened as a museum in 1928 and is today administered by Kode — Bergen's art and design museum network. The museum building houses an exhibition centre, café, and the 200-seat concert hall Troldsalen, built in 1985 to mark the centenary of Grieg's move to the site.

The site has three main components beyond the museum building: the 1885 villa, the small composer's hut built by Grieg in 1891 beside Nordåsvannet lake (where many of his Lyric Pieces were composed), and the gravesite of Edvard and Nina Grieg carved into the rock face above the lake. Troldsalen hosts chamber music concerts throughout the summer season, and lunchtime concerts during July and August draw audiences who can observe the same lakeside view that inspired the composer. Note: Grieg's villa has been closed since 16 August 2025 for essential preservation works — confirm the current status at kodebergen.no before visiting.

Admission at Troldhaugen uses the standard Kode ticket (NOK 200), which also grants same-day entry to all four Kode art museums in the city centre. An all-inclusive ticket (NOK 550) adds return bus transport from the city centre and a concert. Children under 18 enter free. Kode members enter free. The museum is open approximately May to September, Tuesday–Friday 10:00–18:00, Saturday–Sunday 10:00–16:00, closed Mondays. Parking is available free of charge at the site, a 2-minute walk from Troldsalen.

FAQ
How much does Troldhaugen cost?
A Kode admission ticket costs NOK 200 (approx. €17.00) and gives same-day access to all Kode art museums in the city centre. Children under 18 enter free. Kode members enter free. Buy tickets at kodebergen.no.
When is Troldhaugen open?
Summer season (approx. 20 May–14 September): Tuesday–Friday 10:00–18:00, Saturday–Sunday 10:00–16:00, Monday closed. Grieg's villa has been closed since 16 August 2025 for preservation — Troldsalen, the café, and the park remain open. Confirm at kodebergen.no.
How do I get to Troldhaugen from Bergen city centre?
Take Bergen Light Rail (Bybanen) Line 1 to Hop, then follow signs — approximately 20–30 minutes on foot from the stop. Alternatively, bus 67 runs to Hop Sjølinjen. By car, take the E39 south approximately 7 km and follow signs. Free parking is at the site, a 2-minute walk from Troldsalen.
Is the Grieg villa currently open to visitors?
The villa closed for essential preservation works from 16 August 2025. Check kodebergen.no for current renovation status before visiting. Troldsalen concert hall, the composer's hut, the café, and the grounds remain accessible during the museum season.
Where do I buy tickets for Troldhaugen?
Tickets are sold at kodebergen.no. One ticket covers Troldhaugen and all Kode art museums in the city centre on the same day. An all-inclusive ticket (NOK 550, approx. €46.50) adds a return bus transfer from the city centre and concert admission.
