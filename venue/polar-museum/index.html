About

The Polar Museum (Polarmuseet) is located at Sjøgata 1 on the northern waterfront of Tromsø city centre, in a restored warehouse building dating from the 1830s. The building was originally used for cargo handling on the Tromsø harbour and is one of the oldest structures in the city. The museum is managed by UiT — The Arctic University of Norway, which also operates the Arctic University Museum, the MS Polstjerna vessel, and the Arctic-Alpine Botanic Garden.

The museum documents Arctic hunting, trapping, and polar exploration history from the 19th and early 20th centuries. Permanent exhibitions cover Norwegian seal hunting operations, polar bear and Arctic fox trapping on Svalbard and Franz Josef Land, and the history of Norwegian polar expeditions. The ground floor houses The Sea Warehouse, which contains current temporary exhibitions. The first floor (second level in Norwegian usage) is accessible by stairs only — there is no elevator. On-site accessibility aids include audio guides, video guides, hearing kits, and braille descriptions. A small discount is available for wheelchair users who cannot access the upper floor.

The museum is open every day 10:00–18:00, with the exception of 1 May and 17 May (Norwegian public holidays). No guided tours run at fixed times; bespoke guided tours for groups can be arranged by contacting postmaster@polarmuseum.no. The museum has a shop on site. Cruise ship arrivals at the Tromsø port terminal, approximately 200 m from the entrance, have a significant effect on visitor numbers — the museum can become crowded within one hour of a large vessel docking.

Best time: Monday to Wednesday before 11:00 are the quietest periods. The Polar Museum is one of the most cruise-affected venues in Tromsø — the port terminal is approximately 200 m away. On days when a large cruise ship is docked (check tromsohavn.no for schedules), the museum can reach capacity within the first hour of opening. The narrow historic building layout limits flow-through capacity. Arriving at 10:00 on weekdays avoids both cruise groups and general tourist foot traffic on Storgata.
FAQ
What are the opening hours for the Polar Museum?
Open every day 10:00–18:00. Closed 1 May and 17 May (Norwegian public holidays). Verify at en.uit.no/tmu/polarmuseet/planlegg before visiting.
How much does the Polar Museum cost?
2025 prices from en.uit.no/tmu/polarmuseet/planlegg: Adults 120 NOK (approx. €10.50). Seniors 67+, disability, overseas students, and military 100 NOK (approx. €8.50). Under-18 and UiT students free. Annual pass 400 NOK (approx. €35.00), valid at all UiT museum venues for one year.
Where is the Polar Museum and how do I get there?
Address: Sjøgata 1, 9008 Tromsø — northern waterfront of the city centre, walking distance from most hotels. There is no dedicated museum car park; Tromsø City Council operates paid parking nearby. Many bus routes stop close by — use the city centre bus stops and walk to the waterfront.
What does the Polar Museum cover?
The museum documents Arctic hunting, trapping (seal, polar bear, Arctic fox), and Norwegian polar expedition history from the 19th and early 20th centuries. The building is a restored 1830s harbour warehouse. The ground floor houses temporary exhibitions. The first floor (accessed by stairs only) contains permanent exhibition galleries.
Is the Polar Museum wheelchair accessible?
Ground floor only — the upper floor is stairs-only with no elevator due to the historic building structure. A small discount is available at the ticket counter for wheelchair users. For specific accessibility information before visiting, contact postmaster@polarmuseum.no or +47 776 233 60.
