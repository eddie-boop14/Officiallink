About

The Nobel Peace Center is the museum dedicated to the Nobel Peace Prize, housed in a converted former railway station at Brynjulf Bulls plass 1 on Oslo's Rådhusplassen waterfront, 100 metres from the Aker Brygge tram stop. The building was opened as a museum in 2005 and presents exhibitions on all Peace Prize laureates from 1901 to the present. The permanent exhibition Vilje til å endre verden (Will to Change the World) provides biographical and contextual material on every laureate. Temporary exhibitions address current themes in peace, democracy, and human rights — the 2025 prize exhibition, Democracy on the Brink, runs until 30 September 2026.

The museum is fully wheelchair accessible with a ramp at the entrance and a lift between its two floors. Self-service lockers are available free of charge. A free audio guide covering the exhibitions is accessible via visitors' own phones. Guided tours run every weekend and daily during Easter; tours last approximately 30 minutes and accommodate up to 30 people — registration on arrival. The museum does not sell food or drink on site. Oslo Pass holders enter free; discounts also apply for OBOS members and A-Kort holders.

The Nobel Peace Center is closed on Mondays throughout the year, and on specific public holidays including 1 January, 17 May, and 24–25 December. Additionally, 9–11 December — the Nobel Peace Prize ceremony days — are closed to general visitors (the museum shop remains open on 10 December). Summer hours (1 June–31 August) extend daily to 18:00 with opening at 10:00. School groups must book in advance via the museum's education programme.

FAQ
How much does the Nobel Peace Center cost?
Adult: NOK 180 (approx. €16.00). Student: NOK 100 (approx. €9.00). Children 0–17: free. Annual pass adult: NOK 425 (approx. €38.00) — includes guest entry and 10% shop discount. Oslo Pass holders enter free. Book at ticket.nobelpeacecenter.org to avoid reseller markups.
What are the opening hours of the Nobel Peace Center?
Tuesday–Sunday 11:00–17:00. Wednesday extended to 20:00. Closed Mondays. Summer (1 June–31 August): open daily 10:00–18:00. Closed on 1 Jan, 3 Mar, 17 May, 9–11 Dec, 24–25 Dec, 31 Dec. During Easter (28 Mar–6 Apr 2026), open every day. Check nobelpeacecenter.org for the current-day schedule.
Where is the Nobel Peace Center located?
Brynjulf Bulls plass 1, 0250 Oslo, on Rådhusplassen (City Hall Square) waterfront. Tram line 12 stops at Aker brygge, 100 metres away. Bus lines 32, 33, 54, 81, and 169 stop at Dokkveien, 200 metres away. Nationaltheatret T-bane station is 700 metres away.
Do I need to book tickets in advance?
Advance booking is recommended but not required for individual visits — tickets can be purchased on arrival. School groups and private guided tours must book in advance at nobelpeacecenter.org. Book online at ticket.nobelpeacecenter.org to guarantee entry on busy days.
What exhibitions are currently on at the Nobel Peace Center?
Current exhibitions (as of March 2026): 'Democracy on the Brink' (to 30 Sep 2026), the permanent exhibition 'Vilje til å endre verden', and 'WASH in War' (to 19 Apr 2026). New exhibitions 'A City of Ghosts' and 'Paths to Peace' open in April 2026. Full listing at nobelpeacecenter.org.
