About

The Musée national des arts asiatiques – Guimet opened in 1889 on the place d'Iéna in Paris's 16th arrondissement. It holds one of the largest collections of Asian art in the world outside Asia, with over 45,000 objects covering 5,000 years of history across the full range of Asian civilisations — from Afghanistan and Central Asia through India, Southeast Asia, China, Korea and Japan. The collection was originally assembled by Lyon industrialist Émile Guimet (1836–1918) during extensive travels through Asia in the 1870s, beginning as a museum in Lyon before transferring to Paris in 1889. The French state took over management in 1927, refocusing the institution exclusively on Asian arts. Major renovation works carried out between 1996 and 2001 introduced extensive natural lighting and expanded the galleries.

The permanent collection presents more than 15,000 objects across four floors. Highlights include Khmer sculpture from the Angkor period, Chinese lacquerware and porcelain from multiple dynasties, 17th-century Japanese samurai armour, Indian miniature paintings, Afghan archaeological finds, and extensive collections of Buddhist art tracing the religion's spread from India through Central Asia to East Asia. A panoramic rooftop terrace, open seasonally, provides views over the Bois de Boulogne and towards the Eiffel Tower. The Hôtel d'Heidelbach at 19 avenue d'Iéna — a two-minute walk away — houses the Buddhist Pantheon collection and a Japanese garden, included in the same combined ticket. A third affiliated site, the Musée d'Ennery on avenue Foch, preserves a 19th-century collection of Chinese and Japanese objects and is accessible by reservation only.

The full-price ticket is €15, covering permanent collections, current temporary exhibitions and the Hôtel d'Heidelbach. The reduced rate is €12 for eligible categories. Admission is free on the first Sunday of every month for all visitors without reservation, subject to the authorised safety limit. Reservation is not obligatory for individual visitors but is recommended for weekends and during major exhibitions. Tickets are purchased at billetterie.guimet.fr. No booking fee applies online.

FAQ
How much does Musée Guimet cost?
€15 full price, €12 reduced rate. The single combined ticket covers permanent collections, temporary exhibitions, and the Hôtel d'Heidelbach. Free for under-18s, EU residents aged 18–25, and all visitors on the first Sunday of every month. Book at billetterie.guimet.fr. Resellers charge €20–€35 for the identical ticket — there is no added value.
What are the Musée Guimet opening hours?
Open Monday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday and Sunday from 10:00 to 18:00. Ticket desk closes at 17:15, last entry at 17:30, room evacuation begins at 17:45. Closed every Tuesday, 1 January, 1 May, and 25 December. Early closing at 16:45 on 24 and 31 December.
How do I get to Musée Guimet?
Metro line 9 to Iéna station or metro line 6 to Boissière station. RER C to Pont de l'Alma. Buses 63 and 82 stop at Iéna; buses 22 and 30 stop at Kleber-Boissière. Address: 6 place d'Iéna, 75116 Paris.
Is the Hôtel d'Heidelbach included in the Guimet ticket?
Yes — the combined ticket includes access to the Hôtel d'Heidelbach (Buddhist Pantheon collection with Japanese garden), located 2 minutes away on foot at 19 avenue d'Iéna. The Hôtel d'Heidelbach was closed for renovation until January 2026 — check guimet.fr before visiting for current status.
Is the first Sunday of the month free at Musée Guimet?
Yes. On the first Sunday of every month, entry to permanent collections and temporary exhibitions is free for all visitors without reservation, subject to safety capacity limits. This is official museum policy confirmed at guimet.fr. Arriving early is advisable as the museum may close access once capacity is reached. Book a free timed slot at billetterie.guimet.fr to guarantee entry.
