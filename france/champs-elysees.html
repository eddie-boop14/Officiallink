About

The Avenue des Champs-Élysées stretches 1.9 kilometres through the 8th arrondissement, linking Place de la Concorde at the eastern end to Place Charles-de-Gaulle and the Arc de Triomphe at the west. The name translates as the Elysian Fields — the paradise of Greek mythology — and the avenue has served as the ceremonial heart of Paris since Louis XIV had it laid out in 1667 as a royal promenade extending the axis of the Tuileries garden.

The lower section near Concorde is flanked by the Grand Palais and Petit Palais, built for the 1900 Universal Exhibition. The upper section is lined with flagship luxury stores, cinemas, cafés, and the Lido cabaret. The pavement is wide enough that walking the full length is feasible in either direction — the avenue has been traffic-calmed in recent years, with a long-term pedestrianisation plan underway.

The Arc de Triomphe at the western end requires a separate ticket (€16, under 18 free) to access the roof terrace — book directly at paris-arc-de-triomphe.fr. The avenue itself costs nothing and is always open. Every 14 July, the national Bastille Day military parade runs the full length. On New Year's Eve, the avenue fills for midnight fireworks over the Arc.

FAQ
Is the Champs-Élysées free?
Yes. The avenue is a public street — free to walk at any time, 24 hours a day. The Arc de Triomphe at the top requires a separate ticket (€16) to access the roof terrace, booked at paris-arc-de-triomphe.fr. Any site selling "Champs-Élysées access" is running a scam.
How long is the Champs-Élysées?
1.9km from Place de la Concorde at the eastern end to Place Charles-de-Gaulle and the Arc de Triomphe at the western end. A leisurely walk takes about 25–30 minutes end to end.
What is on the Champs-Élysées?
Flagship stores (Louis Vuitton, Cartier, H&M), cinemas, cafés, restaurants, and the Lido cabaret. The Grand Palais and Petit Palais are just off the avenue near Concorde. The Christmas market runs November to January.
When is the best time to visit?
Weekday mornings before 10:00 are the quietest. July 14 (Bastille Day) hosts the national military parade along the full length. New Year's Eve draws large crowds for midnight fireworks over the Arc de Triomphe.
How do I get there by metro?
Metro Line 1 stops directly on the avenue: Franklin D. Roosevelt (midpoint), George V (upper section), and Charles-de-Gaulle–Étoile (Arc de Triomphe end). Concorde (Lines 1, 8, 12) serves the lower end near Place de la Concorde.
