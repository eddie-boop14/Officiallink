📍 🚊 Cité du Vin
La Cité du Vin opened in 2016 in a sculptural building designed to evoke swirling wine in a glass, located on the Garonne riverfront in the Bassins à Flot district. The permanent exhibition spans 3,000 square metres across 20 thematic chapters tracing the history, geography, culture, and civilisations of wine worldwide. Every standard admission ticket includes a wine tasting at the panoramic Belvédère on the 8th floor, with views across the city. The experience combines immersive multimedia installations with physical artefacts — it is designed as a sensory journey, not a conventional museum. Advance booking is strongly recommended, particularly in summer and for weekend visits.
🇫🇷 Bassins des Lumières
Europe's Largest Digital Art Centre — WWII Submarine Base
€12.50
€19–€31
→
📍
Bassins des Lumières occupies four of the submarine pens of the Base sous-marine de Bordeaux, a massive reinforced concrete structure built by the German navy during the Second World War on the left bank of the Garonne. The venue, operated by Culturespaces, projects large-scale digital artworks across the walls, ceilings, and water surface of the pens — each roughly 110 metres long and 11 metres high. The scale is genuinely exceptional; the effect of images reflected in dark water at this size is unlike conventional gallery or cinema experiences. Programming rotates two or three times a year with major exhibitions typically dedicated to a single artist or movement. Advance booking recommended for weekend and holiday sessions.

📍 🚊 Place de la Bourse
The Place de la Bourse was built between 1730 and 1775 as a royal square for Louis XV, facing the Garonne. Its symmetrical facades in pale Gironde stone are among the finest examples of 18th-century French classical architecture. Directly opposite, the Miroir d'Eau is a 3,450 square metre shallow reflecting pool — at 2 centimetres depth when filled — that creates a mirror image of the square. It alternates between flooding, drainage, and mist cycles throughout the day. One of the most photographed spots in France and genuinely spectacular at dusk when the stonework reflects gold. Both the square and the reflecting pool are free and accessible at all times.
🇫🇷 Grottes de Pair-non-Pair
Prehistoric Cave — Original Engravings, 33,000–22,000 Years Old
€13
€20–€32
→
📍
The Grottes de Pair-non-Pair are located approximately 30 kilometres north of Bordeaux near Prignac-et-Marcamps in the Gironde valley. The cave contains original engravings of animals — horses, bison, ibex, mammoths, and rhinoceroses — dating to between 33,000 and 22,000 years ago. Unlike the Lascaux reproductions, these are the actual prehistoric engravings in their original location. The site is managed by the Centre des Monuments Nationaux. Visits are by guided tour only, strictly limited to 18 people per session. Advance booking is essential — tours fill well in advance, particularly in summer. This is genuinely one of the most remarkable prehistoric sites accessible to visitors in France and is significantly undervisited compared to Lascaux or Font-de-Gaume. Allow 1–1.5 hours for the visit.

📍 🚊 Grand Théâtre
The Grand Théâtre de Bordeaux was built between 1773 and 1780 by the architect Victor Louis and is considered one of the finest examples of neoclassical theatre architecture in Europe — its colonnade and interior decor directly influenced the design of the Paris Opéra. The venue hosts opera, ballet, and orchestral concerts under the Opéra National de Bordeaux umbrella, with programming running from September through June. Youth tickets (under 28) are available at reduced rates 48 hours before curtain. Last-minute tickets are available at the box office on performance days.
🇫🇷 Saint-André Cathedral & Pey-Berland Tower
Gothic Cathedral and 66m Detached Bell Tower — 230 Steps
€6
€9–€15
→
📍 🚊 Hôtel de Ville
The Cathédrale Saint-André is the main Gothic cathedral of Bordeaux, begun in the 11th century and substantially rebuilt in the 13th and 14th centuries. The north portal sculptures and the choir are of particular architectural note. Entrance to the cathedral itself is free. The Pey-Berland Tower — the detached bell tower standing 66 metres high — is a separate monument managed by the Centre des Monuments Nationaux and requires a ticket for access. Climbing the tower involves approximately 230 steps with no lift and provides one of the best elevated views over the city rooflines.

🇫🇷 Musée d'Aquitaine
Bordeaux's Main Historical Museum — 2,600 Years
€6/8
€9–€15
→
📍 🚊 Musée d'Aquitaine
The Musée d'Aquitaine traces the human history of the Bordeaux region from prehistoric settlements through Gallo-Roman Burdigala, the medieval wine trade, the Atlantic slave trade, and colonial Bordeaux up to the 20th century. The collection of approximately 80,000 objects includes some of the most significant prehistoric Venus figurines found in France, Gallo-Roman sculptures, and an important section covering the slave trade that does not minimise Bordeaux's historic role as one of France's leading slaving ports. No online ticketing — tickets are sold at the door only on the day of visit. Free entry on the first Sunday of each month (except July and August). Allow 2–3 hours.

🇫🇷 Pont de Pierre
Bordeaux's First Bridge — 17 Arches, 487m, Built by Napoleon
Free
→
⚠️ Needs verify
The Pont de Pierre was constructed between 1810 and 1822 on Napoleon's orders — he reportedly wanted to cross the Garonne without delay on his way to Spain. The bridge has 17 arches, a number said to correspond to letters in the name Napoléon Bonaparte. It connects the city centre to the right bank neighbourhood of La Bastide. Open to pedestrians, cyclists, and limited traffic. Views back towards the quays and the cathedral tower are excellent, particularly in late afternoon light. Free to cross.

🇫🇷 École du Vin de Bordeaux (CIVB)
Official Wine School — Run by the Bordeaux Wine Trade Body
See site
→
📍
The École du Vin de Bordeaux is operated by the CIVB (Conseil Interprofessionnel du Vin de Bordeaux), the official body representing all Bordeaux wine producers. Located in the cours du XXX Juillet adjacent to the tourist office, it offers introductory tastings, themed workshops, and multi-session courses at various price points. This is the most direct and authoritative wine education experience in the city, led by accredited wine educators. The adjacent Bar à Vin serves Bordeaux wines by the glass at reasonable prices. Booking through the official CIVB site.

Official Guided Day Trips — Saint-Émilion, Médoc, Pessac-Léognan
See site
→
📍 UNESCO
The Office de Tourisme et des Congrès de Bordeaux Métropole operates official guided excursions to the main wine appellations surrounding Bordeaux, departing from the tourist office at 12, cours du XXX Juillet. Destinations include Saint-Émilion (UNESCO-listed wine town), the Médoc châteaux corridor, and Pessac-Léognan. Half-day and full-day formats available. Booking through visiter-bordeaux.com is the direct official route. The tourist office excursions are the same price or cheaper and use licensed local guides.

🇫🇷 Miroir d'Eau
World's Largest Reflecting Pool — Place de la Bourse
Free
→
📍
The world's largest reflecting pool, a thin sheet of water covering 3,450 square metres of granite opposite the Place de la Bourse. Designed by landscape architect Michel Corajoud, it alternates between mirror effects (still water reflecting the 18th-century facades) and mist effects (fog rising from the surface). It has become Bordeaux's most photographed attraction since its installation in 2006. Best experienced at sunset or after dark when the buildings are illuminated. Free. Operates March–November. Allow 15–30 minutes.

River Sightseeing — Pont de Pierre, Cité du Vin, Port de la Lune
€14
€21–€35

Several operators offer sightseeing cruises on the Garonne from the Quais de Bordeaux, passing under the Pont de Pierre, past the Cité du Vin, and along the UNESCO-listed Port de la Lune waterfront. Evening dinner cruises and wine-tasting cruises are also available. The perspective from the water reveals the full sweep of Bordeaux's 18th-century quayside architecture. Boats depart from Quai des Chartrons or the Ponton d'Honneur near Place de la Bourse. Allow 1–2 hours depending on cruise type.

🇫🇷 Darwin Ecosystème
Alternative Cultural Quarter — Former Military Barracks
Free
→
📍
Darwin occupies a former military barracks on the right bank of the Garonne in the Bastide neighbourhood, a short walk across the Pont de Pierre from the city centre. The site operates as an open urban ecosystem combining an organic food market, restaurants, a coworking space, a skatepark, and a major programme of cultural events and concerts. The building exteriors and internal courtyards are covered with significant street art commissions. Entry to the site itself is free; the skatepark charges a daily fee. Events are ticketed individually and announced on the Darwin agenda. A weekly organic market takes place on Saturdays. The atmosphere is distinctly different from the classical city centre.
🇫🇷 Quais de Bordeaux (Waterfront Promenade)
4.5km Pedestrian Promenade Along the Garonne
Free
→
📍
The transformation of Bordeaux's waterfront from industrial port to public promenade was the centrepiece of the city's urban regeneration between 1995 and 2013. The 4.5-kilometre stretch from the Pont de Pierre to the Chartrons district is now a continuous riverside park with gardens, a 350-metre skatepark, a swimming pool barge, cycling paths, beach volleyball, and café terraces. The Miroir d'Eau sits at the centre of the quays opposite the Place de la Bourse. This is where Bordeaux lives outdoors — the quality of the public space is comparable to the best in Europe.
🇫🇷 Place des Quinconces
One of Europe's Largest Squares — Monument aux Girondins
Free
→
📍
At 126,000 square metres, Place des Quinconces is one of the largest urban squares in Europe. The central Monument aux Girondins, a 54-metre column topped by a Liberty figure, commemorates the Girondin deputies executed during the French Revolution. Two elaborate bronze fountain groups flanking the column were cast by the same foundry that made the Statue of Liberty. The square hosts fairs, markets, and events throughout the year. The rows of trees (quinconces) give the square its name. Free. Open at all times.
🇫🇷 Grosse Cloche
Medieval Bell Tower & Gate — 15th-Century City Entrance
Free
→
📍 🚊 Saint-James
One of the oldest belfries in France, the Grosse Cloche is a 15th-century gate tower on Rue Saint-James, once the main entrance to medieval Bordeaux. The bell, cast in 1775, weighs 7,750 kg and still rings on the first Sunday of each month and on national holidays. The tower includes a clock with a gilded face and the city's coat of arms. The narrow Rue Saint-James below is one of the most atmospheric medieval streets in Bordeaux. The tower can be visited on guided tours only. Allow 30 minutes.
