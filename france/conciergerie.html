About

The Conciergerie is a medieval royal palace on the western tip of the Île de la Cité, built in the 14th century as part of the Capetian royal residence. The Salle des Gens d'Armes — completed around 1302 under Philippe IV — is one of the largest surviving medieval Gothic halls in Europe, measuring 64 metres long. After the royal court relocated to the Louvre and then Vincennes, the building passed to royal administrators and then to judicial functions. By the 18th century it had become a remand prison attached to the Palais de Justice above.

During the French Revolution, the Conciergerie became the most notorious detention centre of the Terror. Between 1793 and 1794, around 2,600 prisoners passed through its cells before execution, including Marie Antoinette, who was held there from 2 August to 16 October 1793. Her reconstructed cell is the centrepiece of the visit today. The building also held Georges Danton, the Girondins, and eventually Robespierre himself — who was brought here hours before his own execution in July 1794. The original cell locations are marked, and the augmented-reality HistoPad overlays historical reconstructions on the rooms as visitors walk through.

The Conciergerie is managed by the Centre des Monuments Nationaux and is part of the same Île de la Cité complex as Sainte-Chapelle, accessible via a combined ticket (€20) from paris-conciergerie.fr. The official €13 adult ticket includes the HistoPad augmented-reality experience at no extra cost. Any platform charging more for the same timed-entry ticket is a reseller with no added value.

FAQ
How much does the Conciergerie cost?
€13 for adults booked at paris-conciergerie.fr. Free for under-18s worldwide and EU residents under 26. Combined ticket with Sainte-Chapelle: €20. Free on the first Sunday of each month November through March. Resellers charge €18–€36 for the same access.
Do I need skip-the-line tickets?
No. The official €13 ticket from paris-conciergerie.fr is a timed-entry ticket that provides direct access. The Conciergerie rarely has the queue volumes of larger Paris monuments. Any reseller promoting "skip the line" is charging markup for the identical ticket.
What is the HistoPad?
An augmented-reality tablet included with every ticket at no extra cost. It overlays historical reconstructions on the rooms as you walk through — showing prison cells, royal chambers, and the medieval palace as they appeared centuries ago. Available in multiple languages.
Can I see Marie Antoinette's cell?
Yes. A reconstruction of her cell is part of the visit. The original location is marked, and the HistoPad shows the room as it was in 1793 during her imprisonment before her execution on 16 October 1793.
Can I combine the Conciergerie with Sainte-Chapelle?
Yes. The combined ticket (€20) covers both monuments and is available at paris-conciergerie.fr. Sainte-Chapelle is a 5-minute walk within the same Île de la Cité complex. Both are managed by the Centre des Monuments Nationaux.
