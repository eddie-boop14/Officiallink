About

The Catacombs of Paris occupy a section of the former limestone quarry network that stretches beneath the city. The ossuary was created in the late 18th century in response to a public health crisis: the Saints-Innocents cemetery in the city centre had exceeded capacity and was contaminating the groundwater. Between 1786 and 1788, the bones of some six million Parisians were transferred from overcrowded cemeteries into the quarry galleries 20 metres below street level. The transfer continued in phases through the mid-19th century. The ossuary was opened to limited public visits from 1809 and has operated as a monument historique since 1874.

The self-guided circuit follows a one-way route of 1.5 km through the Galerie de Port-Mahon and into the ossuary proper, where bones are arranged in walls along the tunnels. Stone tablets mark street names above, quarry works references, and inscriptions — including moral epigraphs placed by the 18th-century quarry inspector Héricart de Thury, who organised the ossuary's layout. The temperature underground is a constant 14°C year-round. There are 131 steps to descend at the entrance on the Place Denfert-Rochereau and 112 to climb at the exit, located approximately 700 metres away at 21 bis, Avenue René-Coty. The site is not accessible to visitors with motor disabilities or those with cardiac or respiratory conditions.

The site closed in November 2025 for a major renovation project aimed at restoring and maintaining the heritage infrastructure. The planned reopening is Spring 2026 — confirm the exact date at catacombes.paris.fr before planning a visit. Official timed-entry tickets are sold exclusively on the official website, released seven days before the visit date, with a maximum booking window of one week. Any third-party site selling tickets for dates further in advance is operating outside this system.

FAQ
Are the Paris Catacombs open in 2026?
The Catacombs closed in November 2025 for major renovation works and are scheduled to reopen in Spring 2026. Check catacombes.paris.fr for the confirmed reopening date before planning a visit.
How much do Paris Catacombs tickets cost?
When open, adult tickets are €31 (includes audio guide). Reduced rate for ages 18–26 and students is €25. Children aged 5–17 pay €12 (audio guide not included). Under-5s are not admitted. Tickets are sold only on the official website catacombes.paris.fr, maximum 7 days in advance. Resellers charge €40 and above for the same access.
Where do I buy official Catacombs tickets?
Tickets are sold exclusively through the official website catacombes.paris.fr. Advance timed-entry booking is mandatory — walk-up entry is not possible. Tickets are released 7 days before the visit date. Free-rate tickets (proof of eligibility required) are collected in person at the entrance on the day, guaranteed entry even when online slots show as sold out.
What are the Catacombs opening hours?
When open: Tuesday to Sunday 9:45–20:30, last entry approximately 19:30. Closed every Monday. The exit is located at 21 bis Avenue René-Coty, approximately 700 metres from the entrance — the circuit is one-way and there is no return to the entry point.
What are the access and physical requirements?
There are 131 steps down and 112 steps up. Temperature inside is a constant 14°C with high humidity. The site is not accessible to wheelchair users, people with motor disabilities, or those with cardiac or respiratory conditions. Bags must be no larger than 40×30×20 cm — there is no cloakroom. Strollers are not permitted. Photography without flash is allowed; tripods are prohibited.
