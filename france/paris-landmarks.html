Catacombs of Paris
Underground ossuary holding remains of approximately six million people, transferred from overflowing cemeteries 1786–1860. 1.5 km of tunnels beneath the Left Bank. Timed entry only — walk-up not available.
from €31
✅ verified →

🤍
Montmartre & Sacré-Cœur
Hilltop village with cobbled streets, vineyard and artists' quarter. Romano-Byzantine basilica built from 1875 with panoramic views over Paris. Nave free — dome ticketed.
Free
✅ verified →
🌐 sacre-coeur-montmartre.com 📍 Maps
🌿
Père Lachaise Cemetery
Most visited cemetery in the world. Opened 1804 — 70,000 burial plots across 44 ha. Jim Morrison, Oscar Wilde, Édith Piaf, Frédéric Chopin, Marcel Proust. Free entry. Any ticket seller is fraudulent.
Free
✅ verified →
🌐 pere-lachaise.com 📍 Maps
🚢
Seine River Cruise
Sightseeing cruises along the Seine passing under 37 bridges. Main operators: Bateaux Mouches (Pont de l'Alma), Bateaux Parisiens (Eiffel Tower), Vedettes du Pont-Neuf (Île de la Cité). 1 hr day cruise.
from €18
✅ verified →
🎟 bateaux-mouches.fr 🌐 bateauxparisiens.com 📍 Maps
🏟
Stade de France
France's national stadium in Saint-Denis. Capacity 80,000. Built for the 1998 FIFA World Cup. Guided tours cover the pitch, dressing rooms and presidential suite. 2024 Paris Olympics venue.
from €18.50

Opéra Garnier
Beaux-Arts opera house inaugurated 1875. Grand staircase in polychrome marble, foyer with mirrors and chandeliers, auditorium ceiling painted by Marc Chagall in 1964. Self-guided daytime visits available.
from €15
✅ verified →
🎟 operadeparis.fr 📍 Maps
🖼
Musée Picasso
Hôtel Salé, a 17th-century mansion in the Marais. World's largest public Picasso collection — acquired by the French state in lieu of inheritance tax after his death in 1973. Closed Tuesdays.
from €14
✅ verified →
🌐 museepicassoparis.fr 📍 Maps
🌸
Musée de l'Orangerie
Tuileries garden gallery housing Monet's eight monumental Water Lilies paintings in two oval rooms. Monet donated the works the day after the 1918 Armistice as a monument to peace.
from €12.50
✅ verified →
🌐 musee-orangerie.fr 📍 Maps

Fondation Louis Vuitton
Frank Gehry-designed contemporary art foundation opened 2014 in the Bois de Boulogne. Twelve glass sails around a central structure. Rotating exhibitions from LVMH collection. Free shuttle from Place Charles de Gaulle.
from €16
✅ verified →
🌐 fondationlouisvuitton.fr 📍 Maps
⚡
Palais de Tokyo
Europe's largest contemporary art site near the Eiffel Tower. No permanent collection — vast concrete spaces host ambitious temporary exhibitions and installations. Open until midnight. 16th arr.
from €12
✅ verified →
