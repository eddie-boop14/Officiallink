About

The Arc de Triomphe de l'Étoile stands at the centre of the Place Charles-de-Gaulle at the western end of the Avenue des Champs-Élysées. Napoleon commissioned the arch in 1806 following the French victory at Austerlitz, intending it to honour the armies of the First French Empire. Construction began the same year but proceeded slowly; the arch was completed in 1836, ten years after Napoleon's death. His body was carried through it in 1840 on the way to its final resting place at Les Invalides. The arch stands 50 metres high and 45 metres wide — at the time of its completion, one of the largest triumphal arches in the world.

The four main piers of the arch each carry a large sculptural group. The most celebrated is La Marseillaise (formally Le Départ des volontaires de 1792) by François Rude on the right-hand pier facing the Champs-Élysées. The inner surfaces of the arch are inscribed with the names of 558 French generals who served under Napoleon, with those who died in battle underlined. The attic walls list the names of 128 battles and sieges. Beneath the arch lies the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier, interred on 11 November 1920 — Armistice Day. An eternal flame burns continuously and is rekindled each evening at 18:30 by veterans' associations. This area is freely accessible from the underpass without a ticket.

The monument is managed by the Centre des Monuments Nationaux. The rooftop terrace at 50 metres gives a 360-degree view of Paris, including the axis from the Louvre through the Place de la Concorde, down the Champs-Élysées, and west to the Grande Arche de la Défense. The interior exhibition covers the arch's construction history and sculpture programme. The climb involves 284 steps via a spiral staircase; the elevator is reserved for visitors with reduced mobility. The official timed ticket at paris-arc-de-triomphe.fr covers the full monument including terrace access — it is the skip-the-line ticket. Never cross the Place Charles-de-Gaulle roundabout on foot — always use the Passage du Souvenir underpass.

FAQ
How much does the Arc de Triomphe cost?
Low season (October–March): €16 online, €17 at the box office. High season (April–September): €21 online, €22 at the box office. Wednesdays in high season revert to low-season prices. Free for under-18s, EU/EEA residents under 26, and all visitors on the first Sunday of the month from November to March. Book at paris-arc-de-triomphe.fr. Resellers charge €22–€45 for the same timed entry — there is no added value.
What are the Arc de Triomphe opening hours?
April 1 to September 30: daily 10:00–23:00, last entry 22:15. October 1 to March 31: daily 10:00–22:30, last entry 21:45. On Tuesdays, opening is delayed to 11:00 year-round. Closed January 1, May 1 (full day), May 8 (morning), July 14 (morning), November 11 (morning), and December 25. Verify hours at paris-arc-de-triomphe.fr on the day of your visit — partial closures occur for official ceremonies.
How do I reach the Arc de Triomphe safely?
Never cross the Place Charles-de-Gaulle roundabout on foot — it is one of the most dangerous traffic circles in Paris. Use the Passage du Souvenir pedestrian underpass, accessible from the Avenue des Champs-Élysées or Avenue de la Grande-Armée. The nearest station is Charles-de-Gaulle–Étoile, served by Métro lines 1, 2, and 6, and RER A.
How many steps to the top of the Arc de Triomphe?
284 steps via the spiral staircase to the rooftop terrace at 50 metres. An elevator is available but reserved exclusively for visitors with reduced mobility. From the attic room (museum level, reachable by elevator), 46 additional steps lead to the terrace. Allow 30 minutes for a quick visit, up to 1.5 hours at a leisurely pace.
Is the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier free to visit?
The base of the Arc de Triomphe and the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier area beneath the arch are freely accessible via the pedestrian underpass — no ticket required. The eternal flame ceremony is held every evening at 18:30. Access to the rooftop terrace and interior exhibition requires a paid ticket, bookable at paris-arc-de-triomphe.fr.
