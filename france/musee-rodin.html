About

The Musée Rodin occupies the Hôtel Biron, an early 18th-century mansion in the 7th arrondissement that Auguste Rodin rented from the French state in the final years of his life. He shared the building at various times with Matisse, Cocteau, Isadora Duncan and Rilke before negotiating sole tenancy in exchange for donating his entire collection to the nation. The museum opened in 1919, two years after Rodin's death, and has changed remarkably little since — the building, the garden and the collection are displayed as Rodin intended.

The collection spans over 6,600 sculptures, 8,000 drawings and 10,000 photographs, alongside works from Rodin's personal collection — paintings by Van Gogh, Monet and Renoir. The 3-hectare sculpture garden is the centrepiece: gravel paths lead past The Thinker on its plinth, the towering Gates of Hell, and The Burghers of Calais. Inside, the Hôtel Biron's intimate rooms display The Kiss in marble, Eternal Springtime, and the plaster studies that reveal Rodin's working process. A room is dedicated to Camille Claudel, his collaborator and companion.

One ticket (€14) covers museum and garden. The garden-only ticket was discontinued — there is no separate garden-only option. Free for under-18s and EU residents under 26. Free first Sunday of each month from October to March only. One of the more manageable major museums in Paris — rarely as crowded as the Louvre or Orsay, and often accessible without a long queue on weekdays. Advance booking at musee-rodin.fr is recommended at weekends.

FAQ
How much does Musée Rodin cost?
€14 for adults — museum and sculpture garden. Free for under-18s worldwide and EU residents under 26 with photo ID. Free first Sunday of each month from October to March. Resellers charge €22–€55 for the same timed entry. Book at musee-rodin.fr.
Can I visit just the garden?
No — the garden-only ticket has been discontinued. One €14 ticket covers both the museum and the full sculpture garden. The garden contains The Thinker, The Gates of Hell, and The Burghers of Calais.
What are the opening hours?
Tuesday to Sunday 10:00–18:30, last entry 17:45. Closed every Monday, 1 January, 1 May and 25 December. Early closing on 24 and 31 December.
Is a timed entry ticket required?
Online booking is recommended. Timed slots are available at musee-rodin.fr. The museum is calmer than many Paris museums and walk-up entry is often possible on weekdays, but pre-booking avoids any queue risk at weekends.
What is the combined Rodin and Orsay ticket?
A combined ticket covering both the Musée Rodin and the Musée d'Orsay is available at €25 per adult. Check musee-rodin.fr for current availability and booking.
