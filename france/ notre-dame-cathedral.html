About

Notre-Dame de Paris is one of the finest examples of French Gothic architecture in the world. Construction began in 1163 under Bishop Maurice de Sully and the cathedral was largely complete by 1345, though it was embellished across the following centuries. Its west façade, three rose windows, flying buttresses, and the rebuilt spire made it an architectural landmark long before Victor Hugo's 1831 novel brought it global fame. On 15 April 2019 a fire destroyed the spire and much of the oak roof structure known as "the Forest." Over 2,000 craftspeople — stonemasons, carpenters, glassmakers, bell-founders — spent five years on the reconstruction.

The cathedral officially reopened on 7–8 December 2024, with its stonework cleaned, rose windows restored, and a new liturgical interior featuring bronze furnishings and 1,500 solid oak chairs. The rebuilt spire, identical in form to Viollet-le-Duc's 1859 design, rises again over the Île de la Cité. The Towers — with 424 steps, a new double-helix staircase, close-up encounters with the gargoyles and chimeras, and panoramic views over Paris — reopened on 20 September 2025. Tickets are €16 per adult and must be booked in advance online at tours-notre-dame-de-paris.fr — no on-site sales. There is no lift; allow one hour for the tower visit.

Entry to the cathedral is completely free for everyone. An optional free timed reservation at resa.notredamedeparis.fr (same-day only) reduces your wait. Walk-in access is always possible but queues at weekends can reach 2–3 hours. The Treasury (€12, paid on-site) holds the Crown of Thorns and centuries of liturgical objects. Free organ recitals take place on Sundays at 16:00. No third-party platform is authorised to sell cathedral entry — any site charging is operating a scam.

FAQ
Is Notre-Dame Cathedral free to visit?
Yes — completely free for everyone. An optional free timed reservation at notredamedeparis.fr reduces your wait but is not required. Walk-in access is always possible, though queues at weekends can reach 2–3 hours. Any site charging for cathedral entry is running a scam — the official timed slot is free.
What are the opening hours?
Monday to Wednesday, Friday, Saturday and Sunday: 7:50–19:00. Thursday: 7:50–22:00. Last entry 30 minutes before closing. Hours may vary during special celebrations — check notredamedeparis.fr before visiting.
Can I visit the towers and how much do they cost?
Yes — the towers reopened 20 September 2025 with a redesigned route and a new double-helix staircase. Tickets cost €16 per adult. Free for under-18s, EU residents aged 18–25, and Paris Museum Pass holders. There are 424 steps and no lift — not suitable for those with reduced mobility. Book in advance at tours-notre-dame-de-paris.fr — no on-site sales.
How do I get a timed reservation for the cathedral?
Free reservations are available at resa.notredamedeparis.fr. Slots are released same-day only, progressively throughout the day. Walk-in is possible but weekends can mean 2–3 hour queues. No third-party platform is authorised to sell entry — any site charging for cathedral access is a scam.
What else can I visit at Notre-Dame?
The Treasury (€12, paid on-site) holds the Crown of Thorns and centuries of liturgical objects, open Mon–Sat 9:30–18:00 (Thu to 21:00) and Sun 13:00–17:30. Free organ recitals on Sundays at 16:00 — no reservation needed. Tuesday evening concerts at 20:30 are ticketed separately at musique-sacree-notredame.fr.
