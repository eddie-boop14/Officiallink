About

The Louvre is the world's largest and most visited art museum, housed in the Louvre Palace — originally a 12th-century fortress built by King Philip II that was expanded into a royal residence across five centuries. It opened as a public museum in 1793 with fewer than 550 paintings; today it holds over 380,000 objects, of which 35,000 are on display across 72,735 m² of galleries spanning eight curatorial departments.

The collection ranges from ancient Mesopotamia and Egypt through classical antiquity, Islamic art, medieval Europe, and on to 19th-century French painting and sculpture. The three most-visited works — Leonardo da Vinci's Mona Lisa, the Venus de Milo, and the Winged Victory of Samothrace — each draw large crowds. Allow a minimum of three to four hours for highlights; a full visit requires a full day. I.M. Pei's glass Pyramid in the Cour Napoléon, inaugurated in 1989, is the main entrance and a landmark in its own right.

Since 14 January 2026 the Louvre operates a two-tier pricing system: €22 for EEA citizens and residents, and €32 for all other visitors. Free admission applies to under-18s, EEA residents under 26, disabled visitors with one carer, and all visitors on the first Friday of each month after 18:00 (except July and August) and on 14 July. Even free-admission visitors must book a timed slot at ticket.louvre.fr. Any exit is final — plan your visit before you go.

FAQ
How much does the Louvre cost?
Since 14 January 2026 there are two tiers: €22 for EEA citizens and residents, and €32 for everyone else. Free admission applies to under-18s, EEA residents under 26, and all visitors on the first Friday of the month after 18:00 (except July and August) and on 14 July. Even free visitors must book a timed slot at ticket.louvre.fr. The museum does not offer a reduced rate — it is full price or free.
What are the opening hours and closed days?
Monday, Thursday, Saturday and Sunday 9:00–18:00. Wednesday and Friday 9:00–21:00. Closed every Tuesday and on 1 January, 1 May, and 25 December. Last entry is one hour before closing; galleries are cleared 30 minutes before closing. Some rooms may be temporarily closed for restoration — check room closure notices at louvre.fr.
Do I need to book in advance?
Yes. Timed-entry booking is required for all visitors including those eligible for free admission. Book exclusively at ticket.louvre.fr. Street vendors and reseller sites charge inflated prices and tickets from unofficial sources may be refused at the entrance.
How do I get to the Louvre by metro?
Take Métro Line 1 or Line 7 to Palais Royal–Musée du Louvre station. Exit 1 leads directly into the Carrousel du Louvre underground entrance. Exit 6 opens at 8:30 and provides a covered route into the museum — often quicker than the Pyramid queue in bad weather.
What is the best time to visit?
Wednesday or Friday evening (open until 21:00) — crowds thin out after 18:00 as daytime visitors leave. Avoid Saturday and school-holiday weekdays. Arriving at 9:00 on a weekday morning is the next best option. The Mona Lisa room is always crowded regardless of time — visit it first immediately on entry or last before leaving.
