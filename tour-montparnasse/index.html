About

Tour Montparnasse divides Paris. Completed in 1973 and rising 210 metres above the 14th arrondissement, it was so controversial that new skyscraper construction within Paris city limits was banned shortly after its completion. The building remains the only skyscraper in central Paris and is routinely voted one of the ugliest buildings in the world. Visit it anyway — from the top, it is the one place in Paris where you cannot see Tour Montparnasse, and the panoramic views of the city are arguably the finest available, with the Eiffel Tower at eye level and the full sweep of Haussmann's boulevards below.

The 56th floor observation terrace and the 59th floor open-air rooftop offer 360-degree views extending 40 kilometres on clear days. The elevator from ground to 56th floor takes 38 seconds — among the fastest in Europe. Unlike the Eiffel Tower, queues are short, tickets rarely sell out, and the experience is unhurried. The view of the Eiffel Tower from here is better than the view from the Eiffel Tower itself, since the tower is in the picture rather than beneath your feet.

Tickets are €19 for adults, with reduced rates for children. Open daily until 22:30 or 23:30 depending on season. Evening visits are particularly atmospheric — the city lights up below and the Eiffel Tower sparkles on the hour. Located directly above Montparnasse station, it is one of the most accessible major viewpoints in Paris.

FAQ
How much does Tour Montparnasse cost?
€19 day / €23 sunset & night. €9.50 child (4–11). Under 4 free. Book at tourmontparnasse56.com — the only official site. Resellers charge €26–€53.
Do I need skip-the-line tickets?
No. Official tickets include timed entry. The tower rarely has long queues. Resellers charge more for the same access.
Is the view better than from the Eiffel Tower?
Many think so — because this view includes the Eiffel Tower itself. You also see Sacré-Cœur, Notre-Dame, the Seine, and La Défense. The open-air rooftop on the 59th floor is the highlight.
When is the best time to go?
Sunset tickets (€23) offer the best of both worlds — daylight panorama that transitions into illuminated Paris with the Eiffel Tower sparkling. Clear weather days are essential.
How fast is the lift?
38 seconds from ground to the 56th floor — Europe's fastest elevator. The final climb to the open-air 59th-floor rooftop is by stairs.
