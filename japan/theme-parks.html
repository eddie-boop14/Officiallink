Tokyo Disneyland (1983) features seven themed lands including World Bazaar, Adventureland, Fantasyland, and Tomorrowland. Tokyo DisneySea (2001) considered world's most beautifully themed park with seven unique ports — Mediterranean Harbor, Mysterious Island (Jules Verne theme), Arabian Coast, and Mermaid Lagoon. DisneySea exclusive attractions include Journey to the Center of the Earth, 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea, and Fantasy Springs area (opened 2024) featuring Frozen, Tangled, and Peter Pan attractions. Two parks require separate tickets — allow full day per park. Located in Urayasu, Chiba Prefecture, 15 minutes from central Tokyo by train. On-site Disney hotels offer multi-day packages. Advance ticket purchase essential — parks frequently reach capacity. Seasonal events transform parks for Halloween, Christmas, and Easter.
Japan's second most visited theme park in Osaka Bay area. Wizarding World of Harry Potter features Hogwarts Castle walkthrough and Forbidden Journey ride. Super Nintendo World (2021) includes Mario Kart augmented reality ride in Bowser's Castle and interactive Power-Up Bands for in-area gameplay. Upcoming Donkey Kong Country expansion adds further Nintendo content. Hollywood-themed areas include Jurassic Park, Jaws, Spider-Man, and Minion Park. Flying Dinosaur coaster ranks among world's best flying coasters. Shows and character meet-and-greets throughout day. Express Passes significantly reduce wait times but sell at premium. Allow full day minimum. Located near Osaka Bay, 15 minutes from central Osaka by train. Seasonal horror nights and special events. Japan-exclusive attractions and food items not found at other Universal parks.
Barefoot immersive art museum in Toyosu where visitors wade through water, walk through flower projections, and experience body-immersive digital artworks. Different concept from sister museum teamLab Borderless — Planets focuses on physical immersion with water rooms where visitors walk knee-deep through responsive digital koi fish, infinity mirror rooms creating endless reflections, and soft terrain installations that shift underfoot. Garden area features living moss garden with projected seasonal flowers. Shoes removed at entrance — all artworks experienced barefoot. Located in Toyosu, Tokyo Bay area. Originally temporary installation that proved so popular it extended indefinitely. Advance booking essential — timed entry slots sell out days ahead. Allow 2 hours. Photography and video encouraged throughout.
Amusement park at the base of Mount Fuji known for world-record roller coasters and horror attractions. Fujiyama was world's tallest coaster at opening (79m), Dodonpa held fastest acceleration record (1.56 seconds to 180km/h), Eejanaika is a 4th dimension coaster with seats spinning independently, and Takabisha held steepest drop record (121 degrees beyond vertical). Super Scary Labyrinth of Fear regarded as one of world's most terrifying haunted house experiences — entire abandoned hospital building. Naruto and Evangelion themed attractions reflect Japanese anime culture. Thomas Land area for younger children. Clear-day Mount Fuji views from rides create unique backdrop. Located in Fujiyoshida, approximately 2 hours from Tokyo. Park entry free — individual ride tickets or unlimited ride passes available.
One of world's largest aquariums organised around Pacific Ring of Fire concept. Central 9-metre-deep Pacific Ocean tank houses whale sharks — visitors spiral downward around this tank viewing it from progressively deeper levels. Fifteen tanks represent different Pacific Rim environments from Antarctica to Ecuador to Japan's forests. Over 30,000 creatures including manta rays, sunfish, dolphins, sea otters, penguins, and jellyfish. Night aquarium events create atmospheric after-dark viewing. Located in Tempozan Harbour Village alongside giant Ferris wheel. Interactive touch pool allows handling rays and sharks. Allow 2-3 hours. Advance ticket purchase recommended during holidays.
One of world's largest aquariums on Okinawa's northern coast within Ocean Expo Park. Kuroshio Sea tank holds 7,500 cubic metres of water housing whale sharks (multiple individuals), manta rays, and hundreds of fish species visible through massive acrylic panel (22.5m wide, 8.2m tall). Four floors descend from shallow coral reef displays through tropical fish to deep ocean. Outdoor sea turtle pools and manatee enclosure included. Ocean Expo Park surrounding aquarium is free and contains Tropical Dream Centre botanical garden and reconstructed traditional Okinawan village. Dolphin shows in adjacent Ocean Theatre. Located approximately 90 minutes drive from Naha. Allow 3-4 hours for aquarium and park.
Major amusement park in Mie Prefecture with one of Japan's largest roller coaster collections. Steel Dragon 2000 was world's longest coaster at opening (2,479m track length). Acrobat flying coaster, Arashi 4D spinning coaster, and White Cyclone (rebuilt as Hakugei hybrid coaster) among headline rides. Adjacent Nagashima Yuami no Shima is one of Japan's largest hot spring bathing complexes — unique combination of thrill rides and onsen relaxation. Nabana no Sato botanical garden nearby famous for spectacular winter illumination (October-May) — one of Japan's largest light displays. Joy Land children's area. Outlet shopping mall on site. Located between Nagoya and Osaka, accessible by direct bus from Nagoya. Allow full day for park and onsen. Less known to international visitors but hugely popular domestically.
Massive Dutch-themed resort replicating Netherlands architecture at full scale including canals, windmills, churches, and palace. Named after Dutch royal residence. Spread across 152 hectares (larger than Tokyo Disneyland and DisneySea combined). Kingdom of Lights illumination events (year-round, peak October-May) feature millions of LED lights across canal reflections, flower projections, and building facades — one of world's largest illumination events. Attractions include VR experiences, boat cruises through canals, museums, and seasonal flower gardens (tulips in spring). On-site hotels in Dutch architectural style. Brewery producing craft beer. Located in Sasebo, Nagasaki Prefecture, accessible by train or direct bus from Fukuoka. Allow full day. Unique atmosphere unlike any other Japanese theme park.
Indoor theme park dedicated to Sanrio characters — Hello Kitty, My Melody, Cinnamoroll, Pompompurin, and others. Entirely indoors across multiple levels, operating rain or shine. Musical parades, character shows, boat rides through miniature Sanrio worlds, and character meet-and-greet experiences throughout. Lady Kitty House recreation of Hello Kitty's home. Character-themed restaurants serve elaborately decorated food replicating character faces. Gift shops with exclusive merchandise not available elsewhere. Located in Tama New Town, western Tokyo suburbs, approximately 40 minutes from central Tokyo by train. Strong appeal to Sanrio fans of all ages — adult visitors as common as families. Advance ticket recommended as capacity limited. Allow 3-4 hours.
Family-oriented Lego theme park in Nagoya with seven themed areas. Miniland Japan recreates iconic Japanese landmarks (Tokyo Skytree, Osaka Castle, Nagoya Castle, Kyoto temples) from over 10 million Lego bricks with moving elements. Rides include Lego Technic roller coaster, submarine ride viewing real fish in Lego-themed underwater scenes, and driving school courses. Lego Factory Tour shows brick manufacturing process with free brick at end. Adjacent Sea Life Nagoya aquarium (separate or combined ticket). Legoland Hotel on-site with themed rooms. Located on Nagoya waterfront, Aratama area. Designed for families with children aged 2-12. Adjacent Maker's Pier shopping and dining complex. Allow full day for park, shorter if children are older.
