📍
Japan's first open-air sculpture museum, opened 1969. Over 120 sculptures by modern and contemporary artists including Henry Moore, Alexander Calder, Isamu Noguchi, and Niki de Saint Phalle displayed across landscaped grounds against mountain backdrop. Picasso Pavilion houses over 300 works spanning ceramics, paintings, and sculptures. Foot bath fed by natural hot spring allows resting while viewing art. Interactive children's play sculptures. Stained-glass tower climbable via spiral staircase. Gardens planted for seasonal colour across 70,000 square metres. Located along Hakone Tozan Railway — accessible by train. One of Hakone's most popular attractions. Allow 2-3 hours for full exploration.
Hakone's oldest museum (1952) specialising in Japanese and East Asian ceramics from Jomon period to Edo era. Collection includes earthenware, stoneware, porcelain, and tea ceremony wares across chronological display. Building's main attraction is its moss garden (Sekiraku-en) — 130 varieties of moss carpet undulating ground beneath maple trees, connected by stepping stone paths. Moss garden widely considered one of Hakone's most beautiful spots, particularly spectacular during autumn foliage and rainy season when greens intensify. Tea room overlooks garden serving matcha. Located at Gora Station on Hakone Tozan Railway, adjacent to cable car departure. Compact museum — allow 1 hour.

📍
Active volcanic zone created 3,000 years ago by Mount Hakone eruption. Sulphurous steam vents, bubbling pools, and barren rocky landscape create otherworldly atmosphere. Famous for kuro-tamago (black eggs) boiled in volcanic hot springs — shells turn black from sulphur and iron reaction. Local legend claims each egg adds seven years to your life. Reached by Hakone Ropeway from Togendai or Sounzan stations — aerial views of volcanic valley during approach. Owakudani Natural Science Museum explains geological activity. Walking trails around volcanic area (occasionally closed due to volcanic gas levels — check conditions before visiting). Mount Fuji visible on clear days from ropeway and viewing areas. Hakone Ropeway connecting lake to valley is key section of classic Hakone loop route.
Crater lake formed 3,000 years ago in caldera of Mount Hakone. Famous for views of Mount Fuji reflected in its waters — visible on clear days from Moto-Hakone shore and sightseeing boats. Two pirate-themed sightseeing ships (Hakone Sightseeing Cruise) cross the lake between Togendai, Hakone-machi, and Moto-Hakone ports. Separate traditional catamaran boats also operate. Hakone Shrine's red torii gate stands in lake water at Moto-Hakone — one of Hakone's most photographed scenes. Old Tokaido highway cedar avenue and stone-paved road sections preserved along southern shore. Lake forms key section of Hakone loop route connecting ropeway to bus. Morning visits more likely to see Fuji before afternoon clouds develop.
Four-kilometre aerial ropeway connecting Sounzan to Togendai via Owakudani, traversing volcanic valley with panoramic mountain views. Gondola cabins carry passengers over active steam vents, sulphur deposits, and barren volcanic terrain before descending to Lake Ashi. Intermediate stop at Owakudani for volcanic valley visit and black eggs. One of Japan's most scenic cable car experiences. Clear days reveal Mount Fuji throughout much of the ride. Part of classic Hakone loop tour using multiple transport types (train, cable car, ropeway, boat, bus). Hakone Freepass covers unlimited ropeway use. Operations suspended during high volcanic activity or severe weather. Allow 30 minutes for full crossing without stops.

Shinto shrine founded 757 AD dedicated to deities of Mount Hakone, historically worshipped by samurai warriors and travellers on the Tokaido road between Kyoto and Edo (Tokyo). Red torii gate standing in Lake Ashi at shrine's waterfront entrance is one of Hakone's most iconic images — particularly atmospheric in morning mist. Main shrine buildings set within ancient cedar forest on hillside above lake. Treasure house displays shrine's collection of historical swords and armour. Kuzuryu Shrine (Nine-Headed Dragon shrine) on lakeside dedicated to water deity. Approach from lake via stone stairway through towering cedars creates atmospheric ascent. Located at Moto-Hakone alongside lake sightseeing boat port. Free to visit shrine grounds. Allow 30-45 minutes.
Reconstruction of Tokugawa-era checkpoint (sekisho) that controlled travel on the Tokaido highway between Kyoto and Edo from 1619 to 1869. All travellers required inspection of travel permits — system designed primarily to prevent weapons entering Edo and feudal lords' families escaping the capital (held as political hostages). Reconstructed buildings include guard quarters, interrogation rooms, and watchtower using historical construction techniques. Exhibition hall displays original checkpoint documents, travel permits, weapons, and explanations of the strict inspection process. Adjacent section of original Tokaido stone-paved road preserved with cedar-lined path. Located at Hakone-machi near Lake Ashi boat pier. Allow 45 minutes.
Museum displaying 15th-19th century Venetian glass alongside modern Venetian artists, set within European-style gardens. Crystal glass tree installation in courtyard garden sparkles with approximately 160,000 glass pieces. Indoor galleries display Renaissance to Art Nouveau Venetian glasswork including chandeliers, goblets, and decorative pieces. Live canzone (Italian singing) performances in garden café. Glass-blowing demonstration and workshop available for visitors to create their own piece. Gardens planted with seasonal flowers against Hakone mountain backdrop. Located in Sengoku area along bus route between Gora and Lake Ashi. Appeals to visitors seeking gentler experience alongside Hakone's natural attractions. Allow 1-1.5 hours.
