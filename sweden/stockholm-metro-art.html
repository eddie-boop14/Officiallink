About

Stockholm's metro system — the Tunnelbana (T-bana) — operates across three colour-coded lines (Green, Red, Blue) serving 100 stations over approximately 110 kilometres of track. The network is owned by Region Stockholm and operated by SL (Storstockholms Lokaltrafik). Since the 1950s, Region Stockholm has commissioned artists to create permanent installations across the network, resulting in artworks at approximately 90 of the 100 stations. Over 150 artists have contributed paintings, mosaics, sculptures, relief carvings, and installations. The collection is widely described as the world's longest permanent art gallery.

The artwork varies considerably by station. The Blue Line stations, all carved from bedrock, include some of the most dramatic installations: T-Centralen (blue line platform) features intricate blue and white painted vine patterns by Per Olof Ultvedt; Kungsträdgården presents a ceiling of blue and white patterns by Ulrik Samuelson alongside archaeological sculptures; Rådhuset is a raw rock cave painted deep red-orange with large columns. On the Red Line, Stadion has a rainbow-coloured platform by Enno Hallek (1973); Tekniska högskolan features geometric ceiling art. On the Blue Line's northern branch, Solna centrum depicts a panoramic red-orange sunset with politically themed forest scenes by Karl-Olov Björk and Anders Åberg. A 24-hour SL pass allows unlimited station visits across all lines.

SL operates free guided Art Walks in English from June to August, running every Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday at 15:00. Tours depart from the SL Customer Centre at T-Centralen (entrance from Sergels Torg), last approximately one hour, and visit three stations. A valid SL metro ticket is required to travel between stations, but the tour itself is free and no booking is required. Detailed information on current Art Walk schedules and the official metro map are available at sl.se. Conversion rate used: 1 SEK ≈ €0.087 (March 2026). Last updated: March 2026 · Verified

FAQ
How much does it cost to see Stockholm Metro Art?
No separate ticket is required. Access is included with a standard SL metro ticket. A single 75-minute journey costs SEK 43 (approx. €3.75) for adults. A 24-hour pass costs SEK 175 (approx. €15.00). A 72-hour pass costs SEK 350 (approx. €30.50). Buy in the SL app, at station ticket machines, SL Centre offices, or tap a contactless card at the gates. Official source: sl.se.
Are there free guided tours of Stockholm Metro Art?
Yes. SL operates free guided Art Walks in English from June to August, every Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday at 15:00. Tours start from the SL Customer Centre at T-Centralen (entrance from Sergels Torg). A valid metro ticket is required to travel between stations but the tour itself is free. No booking required — arrive at T-Centralen by 15:00. Tour duration approximately 1 hour, visiting 3 stations.
Which Stockholm metro stations have the best art?
Frequently cited stations include: T-Centralen (blue line — blue and white vine patterns by Per Olof Ultvedt), Kungsträdgården (archaeological sculptures, blue and white ceiling by Ulrik Samuelson), Rådhuset (raw rock cave painted deep red-orange), Stadion (rainbow-coloured platform by Enno Hallek), Solna centrum (political forest panorama by Björk and Åberg), and Tekniska högskolan. All are on the Blue or Red line and accessible with a standard SL ticket.
How do I buy SL metro tickets in Stockholm?
Tickets are sold in the SL app, at ticket machines inside every metro station, at SL Centre service offices (including T-Centralen), at convenience stores, and via contactless bank card (Visa, Mastercard, American Express) tapped directly at platform gates. No cash accepted. A single trip covers all transfers within Zone A for 75 minutes. Current prices and routes: sl.se.
How many metro stations have art in Stockholm?
Approximately 90 of Stockholm's 100 metro stations feature permanent art installations created by over 150 artists. The project began in the 1950s when artists were first commissioned to work alongside the metro's construction. The art programme is managed by Region Stockholm via SL. The three lines (Green, Red, Blue) span approximately 110 kilometres of track.
